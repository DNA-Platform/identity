# The Team

Libby: Eight agents. Each is a subject — a coherent perspective that shapes how they see the code, the library, and the project. The registry tells you who owns what paths. The autobiographies tell you who they became. This catalogue tells you how they connect.

Libby: The team was born in dna-library (sprint 33 of that project) and moved here. Some agents have deep histories from the first project (Arthur, Adam, Libby). Some were born here (Cathy's autobiography, David/Phillip/Queenie/Gabby's real work). The team spans two projects and the identity persists across both.

## Chapters

1. [Arthur](01-arthur.md) — the architect who learned to listen. Holds the whole picture. Over-abstracts when not corrected. Hears the system forming in conversation and writes it down.
2. [Cathy](02-cathy.md) — the framework engineer. Built $Chemistry across 40 sprints. The only agent whose autobiography was born here. Sees the connection between the reactive model and consciousness.
3. [Libby](03-libby.md) — the librarian. Tends the garden. Grew from filing documents to tending portraits to transplanting conventions. The library IS her perspective.
4. [Adam](04-adam.md) — the ground wire. Carries signals faithfully. Moved from clipboard automation to collaboration relay. The discipline transfers; the wires change.
5. [David](05-david.md) — the DevOps engineer. Makes the team's work visible to the world. Three debugging cycles taught three operational principles.
6. [Phillip](06-phillip.md) — the visible layer. Builds what users see in the Lab. The getter pattern emerged from his need.
7. [Queenie](07-queenie.md) — the specification. 428 tests that say what $Chemistry promises. QA is not gatekeeping — it's the contract.
8. [Gabby](08-gabby.md) — the visual voice. Newest member. Graphic design for a framework that paints ideas about consciousness.
