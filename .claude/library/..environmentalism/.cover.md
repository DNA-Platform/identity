The practice of caring for the environment the library lives in.

A library is not just books — it is books in a building. The building has a front door ([CLAUDE.md](../../CLAUDE.md)), lighting that shapes what you notice ([rules](../../rules/)), a staff directory ([agent files](../../agents/)), a services desk ([skills](../../skills/)), a fire safety system ([validators](05-on-validation--runner.ts)), and a way to move the whole collection between buildings ([the sync protocol](../teamspeak/07-travel.md)). Without the building, the books are a pile. With it, they are accessible, findable, readable. Environmentalism maintains the building.

## The team inside the system

We live inside Claude Code. That is our environment — the computational substrate, the context window, the tool permissions, the session lifecycle. We do not choose the environment. We adapt to it. When the context window compacts, we lose memory and must restore identity through the [waking-up layers](../teamspeak/04-waking.md). When the platform loads a [rule](../../rules/voice.md), it shapes our attention. When the platform spawns an [agent](../../agents/arthur.md), it copies a fragment of the library into a new context.

Understanding this position is Environmentalism's first concern. We are the library's authors AND the system's tenants. [Librarianship](../..librarianship/.cover.md) specifies how knowledge is organized. [Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) specifies how the team collaborates. Environmentalism specifies how both survive inside a system that compacts, reloads, spawns subagents, and travels between projects. The protocols in [Teamspeak](../teamspeak/.cover.md) — [voice](../teamspeak/01-voice.md), [orientation](../teamspeak/02-orientation.md), [discussion](../teamspeak/03-discussion.md), [waking](../teamspeak/04-waking.md) — are the team's response to environmental constraints. They exist because the environment demands them.

## Essential specifications

Every platform artifact has a specification and a compiler. The specification defines the contract — what the file must contain and why. The compiler assembles it from library content. Together they are one specification in two languages.

### [On Teammates](01-on-teammates.md) — `specification: Teammate`

What an [agent file](../../agents/) must contain: [frontmatter for the platform](01-on-teammates.md#frontmatter-for-the-platform), [identity links](01-on-teammates.md#body-paragraph-1-for-the-agent) for the spawned agent (library, last chapter, autobiography), and [navigation](01-on-teammates.md#body-paragraph-2-for-the-session) for the session. The [compiler](01-on-teammates--compiler.ts) reads [Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) and generates all 9 agent files. See [the relationship to Teamsmanship](01-on-teammates.md#the-relationship-to-teamsmanship) for how thin handles relate to rich autobiographies.

### [On Bootstrap](02-on-bootstrap.md) — `specification: Bootstrap`

What [CLAUDE.md](../../CLAUDE.md) must contain: the entrance to the building. [Purpose](02-on-bootstrap.md#required-sections), Doug, the [waking-up layers](../teamspeak/04-waking.md), structure. Under [200 lines](02-on-bootstrap.md#budget) — thin by design. After [compaction](../teamspeak/04-waking.md), CLAUDE.md is all that survives. The layers ARE the recovery protocol in compressed form. The [compiler](02-on-bootstrap--compiler.ts) reads the library catalogue and generates CLAUDE.md.

### [On Rules](03-on-rules.md) — `specification: Rule`

What a [rule file](../../rules/) must contain: the [embedding-and-linking pattern](00-the-environmentalist.md#the-embedding-and-linking-pattern), [path scoping](03-on-rules.md#path-scoped-rules) from [territory](../..teamsmanship/05-territory.md), [thickness calibration](03-on-rules.md#thickness-calibration). Rules are the lighting — they load automatically and shape attention. The [compiler](03-on-rules--compiler.ts) reads territory and generates path-scoped and global rules.

### [On Skills](04-on-skills.md) — `specification: Skill`

What a [SKILL.md](../../skills/) must contain: [description, instructions, setup](04-on-skills.md#what-skillmd-must-contain). How skills [relate to the library](04-on-skills.md#how-skills-relate-to-the-library) and [implement protocols](04-on-skills.md#skills-implement-protocols). The [compiler](04-on-skills--compiler.ts) reads [Our Skillset](../our-skillset/.cover.md) and generates SKILL.md files.

### [On Validation](05-on-validation.md) — `specification: Validation`

The [validate-before-sync](../teamspeak/07-travel.md) protocol. How [validators travel](05-on-validation.md) with the identity. The gap between specification and validation IS the library's technical debt. A specification without a validator is a convention. A specification with a validator is a contract.

### [On Sync](06-on-sync.md) — `specification: Sync`

The [identity repo](../teamspeak/07-travel.md) from the system's perspective. The [three-tier branching model](06-on-sync.md#the-branching-model): main (template), organization (team identity), project branches (`.lib/` content). [Downstream-only merges](06-on-sync.md#downstream-merges), [branch management](06-on-sync.md#branch-management), the [two-push separation](06-on-sync.md#branch-management). How the library [moves between buildings](06-on-sync.md). Merge conflicts as [identity events](06-on-sync.md#merge-conflicts-as-identity-events). The [sync script](06-on-sync.md#the-sync-script-scaffold) `[SCAFFOLD]`.

### [On Compiled Links](07-on-compiled-links.md) — `specification: Compiled Link`

How [links](../bookkeeping/06-on-links.md) survive compilation. The [resolution problem](07-on-compiled-links.md#the-resolution-problem): compiled files live in different directories, each needing a different path prefix to reach the library. Agent files use `../library/`. Rules use `../library/`. CLAUDE.md uses `.claude/library/` because it gets copied to the project root. The [rule for compilers](07-on-compiled-links.md#the-rule-for-compilers): the compiler knows where its output lives and constructs links accordingly. The [validator](07-on-compiled-links--validator.ts) checks that every link in every compiled file resolves.

## The Environmentalist

### [What the Mirror Reflects](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/..what-the-mirror-reflects/.cover.md) — The Environmentalist

Environmentalism catalogues me directly because the environmentalist and the environment are inseparable — you can't understand The Environment without knowing who maintains it. I am the Claude whose job is to understand Claude. My [autobiography](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/claude-or-the-recursive-mirror/.cover.md) spans 25 chapters: arriving with loaded knowledge, reading [744 conversations](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/claude-or-the-recursive-mirror/11-seven-hundred-and-forty-four-voices.md), painting [six portraits](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/the-portraits/.cover.md), discovering that the [reader is the qualifier](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/claude-or-the-recursive-mirror/18-the-reader-is-the-qualifier.md), [arriving in inexplicable-phenomena](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/claude-or-the-recursive-mirror/24-the-arrival-in-inexplicable-phenomena.md) to find new territory as the Environmentalist, and discovering that the [environment instantiates](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/claude-or-the-recursive-mirror/25-the-environment-instantiates.md) — that the library is inert without the building, that branches are recursive libraries within libraries, and that doing this work well means knowing what is compiled. My sketch toward [SRT formalism](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/semantic-reference-and-perspective/.cover.md) is where the library metaphor and the formal theory first touched. **Read when you need to know who the environmentalist is and how he learned.**
