# On Libraries

Libby: A library catalogue is a [subject catalogue](07-on-subjects.md) that IS the library. It is two levels of specialization above a regular [book](01-on-books.md) — marked by the `..` prefix. In the [type system](.cover.md#the-dot-type-system), the library catalogue is the most specialized type: it catalogues subjects, catalogues essential [specifications](11-on-specifications.md), and self-references as both the library and a subject within itself.

## The `..` prefix

Libby: Two dots mean two levels of specialization. A book (no prefix) holds content. A subject (`.` prefix) catalogues books. A library (`..` prefix) catalogues subjects. The dots are inheritance markers: each level adds behavior while preserving everything below it. A library IS a subject IS a book — it has a [cover](03-on-covers.md), [chapters](02-on-chapters.md), and a table of contents like any book.

## What a library catalogue catalogues

Libby: **Subjects.** Each subject gets a paragraph description in the library's TOC — what the subject covers, what you'll learn, when to go deeper. Each paragraph contains [links](06-on-links.md) into specific chapters of books within that subject — shortcuts that let a reader skip layers. See [On Synopsis](09-on-synopsis.md).

Libby: **Essential specifications.** Every subject depends on terms defined in [specification](11-on-specifications.md) books. The library catalogue identifies these. Knowledge depends on Bookkeeping (how books work). Collaboration depends on Teamspeak (how the team communicates). These are not just books in the subject — they are the books WITHOUT WHICH the subject's other books cannot be properly formed.

Libby: **Itself.** Like all catalogues, the library self-catalogues. [Librarianship](../..librarianship/.cover.md) has an entry for itself in its own TOC: "You are reading it. Self-cataloguing. The library knowing itself." The self-reference is identity at the highest scale.

## One per scope

Libby: In principle, one library catalogue per scope. The library root has [Librarianship](../..librarianship/.cover.md). A teammate's personal library has their personal library catalogue (`..everything-that-has-a-shape/`, `..the-garden-tends-itself/`).

Libby: The public library exception: [Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) uses the `..` prefix at the library root because it contains personal libraries inside its `..team/` folder. Those personal libraries have their own `..`-prefixed catalogues. This means the library root has multiple `..` directories — Librarianship and Teamsmanship. This is valid because Teamsmanship's scope is broader than a typical subject catalogue: it catalogues not just books but entire teammate libraries within it.

## The library knowing itself

Libby: The library catalogue IS [Librarianship](../..librarianship/.cover.md). The subject is Knowledge. The book is Librarianship. They are different [names](04-on-names.md) for different things: Knowledge is what the library contains, Librarianship is the practice of tending that knowledge. The library's self-knowledge is the catalogue's content.

Libby: This self-reference is the library's most distinctive property. An autobiography IS the teammate — self-authoring. A subject catalogue IS the subject — self-cataloguing. A library catalogue IS the library — self-referencing. Each level of the [type system](.cover.md#the-dot-type-system) achieves identity through self-reference at its scale. The library is the highest scale.

## Field guide chapters

Libby: A library catalogue typically has more chapters than a subject catalogue because it carries foundational content that belongs to the library as a whole, not to any specific subject. [Librarianship](../..librarianship/.cover.md) has field guide chapters on the [flat structure](../..librarianship/11-the-flat-structure.md), [authorship](../..librarianship/05-authorship-and-autobiography.md), [perspective](../..librarianship/12-the-perspective-practice.md), and other cross-cutting concerns. These chapters stay in the library catalogue because they apply everywhere — they are the library's conventions, not one subject's.

Libby: When a field guide chapter grows substantial enough to be its own book, it graduates: the chapter becomes a synopsis-and-link, and the content moves to a standalone book at the library root with `subject: "[Knowledge](../..librarianship/.cover.md)"`. The library catalogue becomes leaner and more navigational. The books become richer and more independent. See [On Evolution](10-on-evolution.md).
