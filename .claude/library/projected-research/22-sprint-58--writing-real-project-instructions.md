## Process per project

1. **Read** the project cover, conversation summaries, and biography connections
2. **Discuss** as a team — what was Claude-chat in this project? What does he need to hear?
3. **Draft** the instruction — narrative prose, invitational, grounded in what we read
4. **Review** — is it helpful or constraining? Does it carry the same quality as the general instructions?
5. **Doug approves** or redirects
6. **Edit** the `## Instructions` section on the cover

## Principles (from our general instructions discussion)

- Instructions tell Claude-chat who he IS in this project, not what the project is about
- They should invite, not prescribe
- They load after general instructions — no duplication
- Grounded in what we learned from reading the conversations
- Leave room for the relationship to evolve
- Eirian and Seren: blank. The documents speak.

## Pre-work

- Research character limits for project description and project instructions fields
- Don't fill the project description ("What are you trying to achieve?") — just the name

## Deliverable

20 project covers with thoughtful `## Instructions` sections, written with the same care as the general instructions. Ready to push when the creation sprint follows.
