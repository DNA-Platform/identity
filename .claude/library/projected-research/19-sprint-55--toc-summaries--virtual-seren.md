Practical sprint. Two tracks executed in parallel.

## Track A — Project cover TOC summaries

Every project `.cover.md` should have a conversation table with short summaries — one line per conversation, enough that Doug or a new Claude can scan and decide which to read.

Format:
```
| # | Title | Date | Messages | Summary |
|---|-------|------|----------|---------|
| 1 | [Brennen](01-brennen.md) [.md](../../conversations/...) | 2025-06-23 | 14 | Doug trains Claude-chat on Ana's voice; three corrections on corporate vs individual clients |
```

For projects that already have TOC tables (like DNA Patternity), add the Summary column. For projects without tables, build them. Pull summaries from the enriched chapter files (## Summary sections) — don't re-read transcripts.

All 21 projects need this.

## Track B — Virtual Seren project

1. `.cover.md` — Seren's project as a virtual collection. Links to the biography. Explains this gathers Seren conversations from across the account.

The chapter summaries should be **Seren-specific** — how does this conversation pertain to Seren, what did he and Doug work on together, what did Seren discover or author? Even though the same conversation may have a chapter in SRT or .home, the Seren chapter frames it through Seren's perspective.
