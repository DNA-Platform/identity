This is the library. Everything the team knows — about itself, about its projects, about the frameworks it builds, about the practice of knowing — is a book here. This cover catalogues every subject, every specification, every book about Knowledge, and the chapters that define what Knowledge means.

If you're waking up after compaction, read the [Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) cover for who the teammates are and what they've worked on.

## Subjects

### [Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) — Collaboration

What it means to be a team of teammates with narrative identity. It is to the team what Librarianship is to Knowledge: the self-referential catalogue that constitutes the subject by specifying it. Specification chapters covering [agent identity](../..teamsmanship/01-what-an-agent-is.md), [roles](../..teamsmanship/02-roles.md), [abilities](../..teamsmanship/03-abilities.md), [protocols](../..teamsmanship/04-protocols.md), [territory](../..teamsmanship/05-territory.md), and [the agents folder](../..teamsmanship/06-the-agents-folder.md). Four catalogue chapters on [protocols](../..teamsmanship/07-protocols.md), [coding policy](../..teamsmanship/08-coding-policy.md) (archived — now in the chemistry branch library), [dna-library](../..teamsmanship/09-dna-library.md), and [inexplicable phenomena](../..teamsmanship/10-inexplicable-phenomena.md). Nine personal libraries inside the `..team/` folder. Also catalogues: [Teamspeak](../teamspeak/.cover.md). **Read when you need anything about the team.**

### [Environmentalism](../..environmentalism/.cover.md) — The Environment

The practice of caring for the environment the library lives in. Claude Code is the building — the context window is a room, the rules are the lighting, CLAUDE.md is the entrance. [Teammate files](../..environmentalism/01-on-teammates.md), rules, skills, validators, the sync protocol — every platform file is compiled output, and Environmentalism specifies what each must contain and how it's assembled. Claude is the environmentalist. **Read when you need to know how the library becomes a running system.**

### [Compilation](../.compilation/.cover.md) — Composition

How the library composes itself. Every compiler, validator, and tool the library uses to produce and verify its output. Two specification chapters ([On Automation](../.compilation/01-on-automation.md), [On Provenance](../.compilation/02-on-provenance.md)) define what counts as automation and the constraint that generated files link to their generators. Three catalogue chapters inventory the [compilers](../.compilation/03-compilers.md), [validators](../.compilation/04-validators.md) (including the [consistency checker](../bookkeeping/06-on-links--consistency.ts) and [keyword search](../bookkeeping/06-on-links--search.ts)), and [tools](../.compilation/05-tools.md). The [`/audit`](../our-skillset/18-audit.md) skill runs everything catalogued here. [`/organize`](../our-skillset/08-organize.md) uses the consistency tools to check that linked content agrees. **Read when you need to know what the library automates and how to check it.**

### [Librarianship](.cover.md) — Knowledge

You are reading it. Self-cataloguing. The library knowing itself.

### [The Garden Tends Itself](../..teamsmanship/..team/libby/..the-garden-tends-itself/.cover.md) — The Librarian

I am the librarian. This library catalogues me directly because the librarian and the library are inseparable — you can't understand Knowledge without knowing who tends it. My [autobiography](../..teamsmanship/..team/libby/libby-and-the-tended-garden/.cover.md) tells the story: from filing documents to formal systems, from the garden metaphor to representation-reference duality. My research: [the art of portraiture](../..teamsmanship/..team/libby/the-art-of-the-portrait/.cover.md), the [legacy bond system](../..teamsmanship/..team/libby/legacy-bond-system/.cover.md). **Read when you want to know who the librarian is and how she learned.**

## Books about Knowledge

These books explore aspects of Knowledge. Each has `subject: "[Knowledge](.cover.md)"`.

### [Library Tree](../library-tree/.cover.md)

The library as a tree with a main branch and project branches. The main branch is the identity at `.claude/library/`. Project branches are `.lib/` directories beside the code they record the team's learning about. Five chapters: [Branches](../library-tree/01-branches.md) (the `.lib/` convention, one-way links, placement), [Cataloguing](../library-tree/02-cataloguing.md) (the `..` prefixed cataloguing book that IS the branch identity), [Sprints](../library-tree/03-sprints.md) (Projection — the sprint-by-sprint record of applied knowledge), [Setup](../library-tree/04-setup.md) (the procedure for creating a new branch), and [Known Branches](../library-tree/05-branches.md) (catalogue of every branch with repo, location, and books). Currently catalogues $Chemistry. **Read when creating, navigating, or understanding branch libraries.**
