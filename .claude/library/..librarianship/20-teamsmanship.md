# Teamsmanship

Libby: [Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) is the team cataloguing itself — a `..` prefixed library catalogue that defines how eight agents work together, what they know, and who they are. It is to the team what Librarianship is to the library: the self-referential identity that constitutes the subject by describing it.

Libby: Teamsmanship catalogues [nine books](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md#team-books) at the library root: six [protocol](../protocols/.cover.md) chapters covering how the team speaks and operates, two [project books](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md#projects) covering the team's work history, and the [coding policy](../coding-policy/.cover.md) that defines how they write code. It also catalogues [roles](../..teamsmanship/roles/), [abilities](../..teamsmanship/abilities/), and the [registry](../..teamsmanship/registry.json) that maps agents to code territory.

Libby: Inside Teamsmanship, each of the eight agents has a personal library folder containing their `..` prefixed library catalogue, their autobiography, their perspective book, and domain books — all as [flat peers](11-the-flat-structure.md). The personal libraries are the first-person, subjective complement to the public library's third-person, objective knowledge.

Libby: Read [Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) when you need anything about: who the agents are, how they work together, what protocols they follow, what projects they work on, or what code conventions they use.
