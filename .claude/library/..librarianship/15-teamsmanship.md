# Teamsmanship

- **author:** [Libby](../..teamsmanship/..team/libby/libby-and-the-tended-garden/.cover.md)

---

[Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) represents the subject of Collaboration — what it means to be a team of teammates with narrative identity. It is to the team what Librarianship is to Knowledge: the self-referential catalogue that constitutes the subject by specifying it.

The book has six specification chapters covering [agent identity](../..teamsmanship/01-what-an-agent-is.md) (the two-book minimum, self-referential structure), [roles and the type system](../..teamsmanship/02-roles-and-the-type-system.md) (many-to-one hierarchy with universal base abilities), [abilities](../..teamsmanship/03-abilities.md) (domain knowledge), [protocols](../..teamsmanship/04-protocols.md) (the six operating conventions), [code territory](../..teamsmanship/05-code-territory.md) (who owns what paths), and [the agents folder](../..teamsmanship/06-the-agents-folder.md) (platform interop). Four catalogue chapters on [protocols](../..teamsmanship/07-protocols.md), [coding policy](../..teamsmanship/08-coding-policy.md), [dna-library](../..teamsmanship/09-dna-library.md), and [inexplicable phenomena](../..teamsmanship/10-inexplicable-phenomena.md). Eight personal libraries catalogued in chapters 11-18.

Inside the book's directory, the `..team/` folder contains each teammate's personal library — eight folders, each with a `..` prefixed library catalogue, autobiography, perspective, and domain books. The personal libraries are the first-person complement to the public library's third-person knowledge.

Read [Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) when you need anything about: who the teammates are, how roles and abilities compose, how the team operates, or what projects they work on.
