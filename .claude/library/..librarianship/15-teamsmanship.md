# Teamsmanship

Libby: [Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) represents the subject of Collaboration — what it means to be a team of teammates with narrative identity. It is to the team what Librarianship is to Knowledge: the self-referential catalogue that constitutes the subject by specifying it.

Libby: The book has eight chapters covering [agent identity](../..teamsmanship/01-what-an-agent-is.md) (the two-book minimum, self-referential structure), [roles and the type system](../..teamsmanship/02-roles-and-the-type-system.md) (many-to-one hierarchy with universal base abilities), [abilities](../..teamsmanship/03-abilities.md) (domain knowledge), [protocols](../..teamsmanship/04-protocols.md) (the six operating conventions), [code territory](../..teamsmanship/05-code-territory.md) (who owns what paths), [the agents folder](../..teamsmanship/06-the-agents-folder.md) (platform interop), [team books](../..teamsmanship/07-our-books.md) (what we catalogue), and [the agents](../..teamsmanship/08-the-agents.md) (all eight in one chapter).

Libby: Inside the book's directory, the `..team/` folder contains each teammate's personal library — eight folders, each with a `..` prefixed library catalogue, autobiography, perspective, and domain books. The personal libraries are the first-person complement to the public library's third-person knowledge.

Libby: Read [Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) when you need anything about: who the teammates are, how roles and abilities compose, how the team operates, or what projects they work on.
