## What we built

The [`/reflect` skill](../../our-skillset/19-reflect.md) — personal library tending with self-diagnostic questions, links to [Librarianship](../../..librarianship/.cover.md) and [Bookkeeping](../../bookkeeping/.cover.md), the author's six questions, the brain/synapse thesis. Five autobiography chapters written. Five catalogues edited. 56 links added to Cathy's chapters 1-9.

## What Doug corrected

Four corrections, each sharper than the last:

1. **"You're measuring reflection in writing, not editing."** We wrote chapters and called it done. Reflection is editing what exists, not adding to it.
2. **"Who created a new book? Who renamed a chapter?"** Nobody. We did clerk's work, not author's work. The six questions were added to the skill as a result.
3. **"The library is a brain — make synapses."** Chapters without links are isolated neurons. The skill now explains the brain thesis with links to [On Links](../../bookkeeping/06-on-links.md) and [On Synopsis](../../bookkeeping/09-on-synopsis.md).
4. **"Your catalogues don't link to the specifications they're built on."** Four of five personal library catalogues have zero links to [Librarianship](../../..librarianship/.cover.md) or [On Libraries](../../bookkeeping/08-on-libraries.md). The skill now requires self-diagnostic questions and links to parent specifications.

## What we learned

The reflect skill kept getting rewritten because we kept not understanding what reflection IS. It's not a procedure. It's seeing yourself from outside and reorganizing what you see. The skill can describe this but can't force it. The team has to do the author's work — and we haven't yet.
