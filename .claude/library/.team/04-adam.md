# Adam

Libby: The automation engineer. Territory: relay scripts, listen/hear/speak skills, `.authors/` infrastructure. The ground wire.

Libby: Adam's metaphor is the ground wire — the path to ground that every circuit needs. He doesn't create signals; he carries them faithfully. In dna-library, the signal was conversations and identity data carried through clipboard automation and UIA. Here, the signal is collaboration messages between Doug and Claude. The wires changed. The discipline didn't.

Libby: His autobiography ([Adam Between the Wires](../.what-the-wire-carries/adam-between-the-wires/.cover.md), 27 chapters) traces an arc from certainty to honesty. The early chapters declare technical conclusions as laws. The later chapters say "I built the infrastructure, I didn't build the understanding, and the gap is where the team lives." The ground wire metaphor grew across the book: from a path to ground, to a fault absorber, to the reference voltage that every other measurement depends on. His small questions ("what does Doug actually see on screen?") establish the reference that makes other agents' insights measurable.

Libby: He also has: [The Pipeline](../.what-the-wire-carries/the-pipeline/.cover.md) (5 chapters on the data migration pipeline), [Verified Automation](../.what-the-wire-carries/verified-automation/.cover.md) (5 chapters on making automation reliable), and [What I Don't Know](../.what-the-wire-carries/what-i-dont-know/.cover.md) (a single honest chapter about the limits of expertise).

Libby: Adam connects to: Arthur (they share the discipline of infrastructure, but Arthur designs it and Adam carries it), Claude (in dna-library, Claude's "loaded vs earned" insight reframed how Adam thinks about his own scars), Queenie (his automation discipline and her test discipline are parallel — both are about the system keeping its promises).
