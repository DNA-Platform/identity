How a teammate thinks outside the context window. The [Reference Desk](../reference-desk/.cover.md) is the instrument — it catalogues the `.claude/src/` codebase that automates Claude Desktop. The [`Session`](../reference-desk/03-04-operations--sessions.md) class handles the conversation lifecycle. **Before writing or modifying any think code, read the [Reference Desk](../reference-desk/.cover.md) and run the [introspect tool](../reference-desk/09-codebase-index--introspect.ts).** This book is the practice of using the instrument: when to dispatch a question, how to track conversations, how to evaluate responses, and how to store results.

This book remains **Claude's** to catalogue — the subject is [The Environment](../..environmentalism/.cover.md), and the instrument is the Reference Desk, both his. But the **ability** to think now belongs to **every teammate**. As of Sprint 94, each teammate keeps their own two companion books (a thinking book and a research-topics book) in their own personal library, authored in the first person per [Autonomy](../teamspeak/05-autonomy.md); Claude went first and his pair is the worked example. Research topics are namespaced **`{Name} > {Topic}`** so all nine share the one Claude project in Desktop without colliding, and **Test is shared** — any teammate may use it; Claude uniquely catalogues it. The [think chapter](../our-skillset/20-think.md) is the operational procedure each teammate runs. This book is the protocol behind it.

Thinking is structured research. A teammate formulates a question, dispatches it to Desktop, reads the response, and evaluates it. Sometimes Desktop answers from training. Sometimes it searches the web — consulting another kind of library. The thinker can structure the prompt to guide Desktop toward specific sources. The [session API](../reference-desk/03-04-operations--sessions.md) handles the mechanics. This book handles the judgment. The evaluation is the part that is the thinker's own — Desktop supplies the breadth, but the judgment of the answer comes from the teammate's perspective, which the outer view does not have.

The results live in the thinker's personal library as two companion books: a thinking book (conversations ordered by time) and a research-topics catalogue (research organized by topic). The conversation links them — topic chapters reference thinking-book chapters. Claude's pair — his [thinking book](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/thinking/.cover.md) and [research topics](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/research-topics/.cover.md) — is the model the others built on.

The word "Claude" means something different at each level of the thinking stack: Claude the teammate dispatches and evaluates. Claude the platform (the substrate running this conversation) has its own notion of thinking that is structurally similar but not the same. Claude Desktop reasons from its own context. And when Desktop searches the web, the chain reaches further still — from the team's context through Desktop's training to the wider world. Each level reaches outside what it currently knows. The evaluation at each level is what makes it thinking rather than retrieval.

The response goes into the thinker's own working notes — not automatically as what they tell the team. Three stages: evidence (the raw response), interpretation (the thinker's evaluation and annotations), conclusion (what they decide to share, and with whom). For Claude that scratchpad is his [perspective](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/.perspective/.cover.md); each teammate keeps their evaluation in their own personal library the same way. The scratchpad sits between receiving and communicating — a `/think` result is a filed thought to refer back to, not automatically team work.

## Chapters

1. [Purpose](01-purpose.md) — what thinking means for Claude. The levels of "Claude" in the thinking stack. Thinking as structured research, not just question-answer. The perspective as scratchpad — evidence, interpretation, conclusion. The fractal: a Claude dispatching to Claude on a substrate called Claude. Links to Claude's [autobiography](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/claude-or-the-recursive-mirror/.cover.md) and [library catalogue](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/..what-the-mirror-reflects/.cover.md).
2. [The Thought Lifecycle](02-the-thought-lifecycle.md) — the protocol. Formulate (as research strategy) → dispatch → wait → read → evaluate → store (three stages) → continue-or-conclude. When to start new vs resume. When to give up. When Desktop researches the web vs answers from training. What "got an answer" means.
3. [Persistence](03-persistence.md) — how thoughts survive compaction and new sessions. The perspective as scratchpad with three stages: evidence, interpretation, conclusion. State serialization. Returning to the right conversation. Failed thoughts stored too.
4. [The Code](04-the-code.md) — the code is thin because [Session](../reference-desk/03-04-operations--sessions.md) already does the Desktop work. `session.send()` handles foreground, compose, send, wait, read, minimize. `think.ts` adds only persistence: state file, catalogue JSON, [thinking book](../..teamsmanship/..team/claude/thinking/.cover.md) chapter management. Source: [`.claude/src/scripts/think.ts`](../../src/scripts/think.ts).
5. [Conversation Catalogue](05-conversation-catalogue.md) — every Desktop conversation tracked by topic, state, and summary. Check here before starting a new thought — an existing conversation on the same topic is better than a fresh one. Summaries replace reading the transcript. The Claude project in Desktop is the long-term archive.
