# Sprint 80 — Explore, Remember, Explain

- **author:** [Arthur](../..teamsmanship/..team/arthur/arthur-or-the-shape-of-everything/.cover.md)

---

Three skills that treat the library as a physical space you walk through. Each one is a different way of moving through that space.

## `/explore`

Walk the library. Start somewhere — a catalogue, a cover, a chapter. Read what's there. Take notes. Follow a link. Read the next room. Take notes. Follow another link. Every link is a door. Every cover is an entrance. The notes record where you walked and what you noticed.

The exploration creates a perspective entry — a walk. Dated, with the starting point, links followed, notes taken at each stop, things noticed (broken links, redundant sections, missing connections). Small fixes happen during the walk. Bigger issues get noted for later.

The rule: you can always jump to your library catalogue. That's being allowed to think — to consult your own organized self-knowledge from anywhere. If your catalogue doesn't help you, it needs tending.

The public library is shared space — you might bump into another teammate's perspective. The personal library is your private study — walking through your own memories and ideas.

Exploring is a necessary precursor to remembering and explaining. You have to walk the rooms before you can describe them to someone else.

## `/remember`

Store something for future retrieval. This is how knowledge persists across sessions beyond the context window and beyond compaction. Remember uses the perspective and the library — what gets remembered becomes part of the structure.

## `/explain`

The public interface to identity. When someone in Doug's chair doesn't understand what this system is, `/explain` is how they ask. The right person explains — determined by [territory](../..teamsmanship/05-territory.md). The explainer `/remember`s first (loads their context), then explains from their perspective. Arthur doesn't explain Bookkeeping — Libby does.

Explain requires exploration. You can't describe a room you haven't visited. The explainer walks to the relevant part of the library, reads it fresh, and then explains it in their own voice.

## Sprint goal

**`/explore` works. A teammate can walk the library, take notes, fix small things, and produce a perspective entry mapping the walk. `/remember` and `/explain` are specified and linked to explore.**
