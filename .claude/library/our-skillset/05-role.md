# role

- **author:** [Adam](../..teamsmanship/..team/adam/adam-between-the-wires/.cover.md)

---

`/role` creates or updates a role definition. A role is a context-loading strategy — a lens that shapes what you notice, what you prioritize, and what you generate. Roles are many-to-one with agents: an agent can hold multiple roles, and a role describes a way of thinking, not a person.

Use it when the team needs a new perspective on code — a new domain of expertise that no existing role covers. The `/sprint` skill recommends new roles when a sprint involves unfamiliar territory. Outside of sprints, use it to formalize a perspective the team has been using informally.

Roles connect to abilities (domain knowledge documents) and to agents (who holds the lens). This skill builds the role definition; the [Teamsmanship](../..teamsmanship/.cover.md) catalogue describes the type system they form.
