# discuss

`/discuss` starts a team discussion. The team talks TO each other — not to Doug, not as a report, but as people thinking together. The skill implements the [discussion protocol](../teamspeak/04-discussion-as-work.md) and respects the [autonomy protocol](../teamspeak/07-autonomy-and-authorship.md): each voice speaks from their own perspective.

Use it when: Doug asks the team to discuss, when a decision spans multiple territories, when the library reveals an incoherence, or after tending (the [tending protocol](../teamspeak/08-tending-your-library.md) ends with discussion).

A good discussion has at least three voices, responds to what the previous person said, includes honest disagreement, and produces ideas no single person would have generated alone. The discussion surfaces insights. The insights get written into the library afterward.
