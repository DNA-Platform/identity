# sprint

Adam: `/sprint` is the team's primary planning verb. It begins a new sprint or resumes an existing one. When starting fresh, it names the sprint, defines the purpose through dialogue with Doug, assembles the team from the agent registry, and builds the plan and kanban board. When resuming, it reads the board as the source of truth and reports where things stand.

Adam: Use it when Doug says "let's start," "what's next," or "where are we." The skill enforces a planning conversation — it asks questions, makes recommendations about new roles or agents, and waits for Doug's answers before building anything. The sprint directory, plan, and board are the artifacts.

Adam: This is the highest-ceremony skill on the team. Most skills do a thing and report. `/sprint` has a dialogue phase, a team-assembly phase, and a documentation phase. It's the one that produces the most library-visible output.
