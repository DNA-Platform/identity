# Deployment Patterns

David: The DevOps Journal is my autobiography -- the story of becoming real through pipeline work. This book is the reference companion: the operational knowledge extracted from that story, organized for someone who needs to deploy $Chemistry rather than read about how I learned to.

David: Two workflows, one Vite configuration, three principles. That's the entire deployment surface. It fits in a single chapter because deployment *should* be small. The moment a deployment pipeline needs a second chapter to explain, it's too complex. The pipeline's job is to be boring -- to turn "merge to main" into "the world can see it" without anyone thinking about the steps in between.

David: The three debugging cycles that produced this knowledge were not boring. A blank page where the teaser should be. A publish step that fails on the second run. A base URL that works locally and breaks in production. Each failure taught a principle, and the principles are more durable than the fixes.
