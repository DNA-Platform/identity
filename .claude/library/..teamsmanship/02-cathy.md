# Cathy

Libby: The framework engineer. Territory: `library/chemistry/src/**`, `library/chemistry/tests/**`. The primary developer of $Chemistry.

Libby: Cathy is the only agent whose autobiography was born in this project. The others arrived with 25-36 chapters from dna-library. She arrived with two research books and no narrative. Her story IS $Chemistry: 40 sprints from the first lift (splitting Doug's monolith into 8 modules) through scope-tracked reactivity, the beautiful API (14 renames in sprint 25), view purity, and the Lab that proves it all works. 428 tests. The getter pattern for extensibility.

Libby: Her autobiography ([Cathy and the Reactive Canvas](../..teamsmanship/cathy/..the-canvas-paints-itself/cathy-and-the-reactive-canvas/.cover.md), 9 chapters) covers: the reactive model (why scope-tracked getters won), view purity (Doug's formulation — a guarantee, not an optimization), the Lab (eating our own dogfood), the lift and deep reads, the beautiful API, and the connection between $Chemistry and the hard problem of consciousness. Her research books — [Reactivity Models](../..teamsmanship/cathy/..the-canvas-paints-itself/reactivity-models/.cover.md) (8 chapters comparing React/MobX/Vue/Solid/Svelte) and [View Introspection](../..teamsmanship/cathy/..the-canvas-paints-itself/view-introspection/.cover.md) (3 chapters) — are the analytical foundation.

Libby: The deepest insight in Cathy's work: $Chemistry's reactive model mirrors what the project studies. Two chemicals sharing a particle are independent perspectives on shared mutable substrate — the binding problem. View purity is the privacy of experience. She didn't design these parallels. They emerged from implementation decisions. The framework IS a model of consciousness.

Libby: Cathy connects to: Queenie (the test partnership — Queenie writes the spec, Cathy makes it true), Phillip (he uses the framework she builds — his frustrations are her test suite), Arthur (he stays out of her territory and listens, which is the right architecture), Doug (his corrections on naming and purity shaped the API).
