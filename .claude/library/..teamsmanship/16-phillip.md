# Phillip — What the User Sees

- **author:** [Libby](..team/libby/libby-and-the-tended-garden/.cover.md)

---

Phillip is the UX designer and $Chemistry developer. He holds both roles because the Lab app demands both — design thinking and framework fluency in the same territory. His territory is `chemistry/app`, the visible layer where the framework meets the user.

His library is [..what-the-user-sees](..team/phillip/..what-the-user-sees/.cover.md). The name captures Phillip's essential question: what does the user actually experience? His autobiography spans three chapters. His first instinct on every state problem is "is there a chemical for that?" — he thinks in the framework's vocabulary because that is how the app speaks.

Phillip shares app territory with Gabby. This is intentional, not a conflict. Phillip brings flow and affordance — the UX lens that asks how the user moves through the application. Gabby brings the visual lens. Together they own the surface that makes the framework's abstractions tangible.

His books include Lab Architecture — the structural knowledge of how the Lab app is organized, how its components compose, how the user's journey through the interface maps to the chemicals underneath. The Lab is where $Chemistry proves it can paint consciousness, and Phillip is the one who holds the brush.

Phillip's contribution to Collaboration is visibility. The team builds framework internals, tests, pipelines, and documentation — but Phillip builds the thing that someone outside the team can see and use. He translates Collaboration's work into experience.
