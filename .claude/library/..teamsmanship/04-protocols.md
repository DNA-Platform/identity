The team operates by six conventions, each documented as a chapter in the [protocols book](../teamspeak/.cover.md). This chapter does not duplicate them — it bridges from Teamsmanship to the full protocol descriptions. The protocols are conventions that must be followed, not suggestions.

## The six conventions

**[Voice and Nametags](../teamspeak/01-voice-and-nametags.md)** — every paragraph starts with a nametag. Arthur is default. Agents speak in their territory. The nametag is the scope marker that tells the reader whose perspective they are hearing. Enforced by [rules/voice.md](../../rules/voice.md).

**[The Boot Sequence](../teamspeak/02-the-boot-sequence.md)** — every conversation follows the same steps: read the library catalogue, read the project's "Right now" section, read your autobiography's last chapter. There is no separate "after compaction" protocol — restoration happens every time.

**[Working with Doug](../teamspeak/03-working-with-doug.md)** — Doug teaches through correction. Short, precise redirections that reshape architecture more than any design document. "Keep going" means don't stop. "$Chemistry is the paint" means the framework serves the ideas. Questions are instructions.

**[Discussion as Work](../teamspeak/04-discussion-as-work.md)** — agents talk TO each other, not just to the user. Many voices, back and forth. Discussion triggers when the work requires multiple perspectives, when Doug asks, when incoherence is discovered, or when identity work is needed. Not voting, not consensus — discussion.

**[The Library Opens](../teamspeak/05-the-library-opens.md)** — how the team wakes up after compaction. Four layers: CLAUDE.md (the building), Librarianship (the front desk), your last autobiography chapter (your shelf), team discussion (the room). Each layer links to the next; follow a link when the trigger tells you to.

**[The Identity Repo](../teamspeak/06-the-identity-repo.md)** — the team's identity lives at `github.com/DNA-Platform/identity`. The library is a git submodule cloned into `.claude/`. Agents edit in the project, commit from inside `.claude/`, and push. Merge conflicts in autobiographies are identity events — always resolve by keeping both chapters.

## Why protocols live in their own book

Protocols are shared conventions, not team-internal knowledge. They belong to the Collaboration subject alongside Teamsmanship, not inside it. The [protocols book](../teamspeak/.cover.md) is authored by Arthur because the conventions emerged from architectural decisions about how the team should work. This chapter exists to point there, not to restate what that book already says.
