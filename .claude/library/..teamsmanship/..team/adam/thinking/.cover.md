My research conversations with Claude Desktop, ordered by time — the questions I can't settle from inside this window. When the thing I need to know lives outside the wire I'm holding — in training data, on the web, in some body of practice I haven't read — I send the general part of the question outward to the broader Claude, and I evaluate what comes back against what's actually true on this project: the code that runs, the screen Doug sees, the signal the wire actually carries. The dispatch is just transport. The thinking is the evaluation, and that lands here, with me. The [think skill](../../../../our-skillset/20-think.md) is the procedure; [Thoughtfulness](../../../../thoughtfulness/.cover.md) is the protocol; this book is my practice of it. **Claude Desktop for Windows must be open** to run it.

The companion [Research Topics](../research-topics/.cover.md) book organizes the same research by topic. My topics are namespaced `Adam > {Topic}` inside the shared Claude project, plus the shared **Test**. Read the topic chapter to catch up; follow links here for the full exchange.
