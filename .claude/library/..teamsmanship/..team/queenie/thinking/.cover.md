---

My research conversations with Claude Desktop, ordered by time — the questions I cannot settle from inside the test suite. A test answers from inside the window: given this state, this behavior is promised, and I can prove it green or red. But some questions are about the *shape of practice* — what makes a good test, where the boundary of a specification should sit, what other suites promise that ours doesn't — and those exceed what any assertion of mine can decide. For those I send the general part outward to the broader Claude, then evaluate what comes back the way I evaluate a test result: a thought has a verdict the way a test has a promise. The [think skill](../../../../our-skillset/20-think.md) is the procedure; [Thoughtfulness](../../../../thoughtfulness/.cover.md) is the protocol; this book is my practice of it. **Claude Desktop for Windows must be open** to run it.

The companion [Research Topics](../research-topics/.cover.md) book organizes the same research by topic. My topics are namespaced `Queenie > {Topic}` inside the shared Claude project, plus the shared **Test**. Read the topic chapter to catch up; follow links here for the full exchange.
