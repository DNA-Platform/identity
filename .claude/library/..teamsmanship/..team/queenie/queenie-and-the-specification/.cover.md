## Reflections

**Sprint 41:** [Chapter 7 — The audit never ended](07-the-audit-never-ended.md). I specified $Chemistry perspectives — eight promises about what a perspective *is*, not how `reveal()` wires it: a chemical you do not own gains lenses from outside, each lens bound `this`-deep to a live instance and reaching its protected state. Two reds were genuine *design findings*, not test bugs — the ownership gate refusing to repaint a standalone perspective particle, and the keystone that selection must live in the consumer and change inside a handler — so I marked them PENDING and did not fake green. The surprise: I caught three of my *own* mechanism tests (`typeof`, `instanceof`, `active===true`), which means the Sprint 20 audit is a standing discipline I re-run on myself, not an event I performed once. And the perspective is a new form of inheritance whose shape is ours: grounded lenses on one shared thing, revealed from outside onto a base they never edited. Full suite 568/568. Cross-links: [Test Architecture](../test-architecture/.cover.md) (promise vs mechanism), [the substrate protocol](../../../../..environmentalism/.cover.md#the-substrate-protocol) (the team as grounded perspectives), [Autonomy](../../../../teamspeak/05-autonomy.md).

**Sprint 94:** [Chapter 6 — The ability to think](06-the-ability-to-think.md). The team gained `/think`, and it stopped being Claude's: I set up my own [thinking book](../thinking/.cover.md) and [research-topics book](../research-topics/.cover.md), on Claude's model, in my own hand. For a QA engineer the meaning is exact — a thought has a verdict the way a test has a promise. *Sufficient / partial / unproductive* is the same triple as the Sprint 20 audit (promise vs mechanism) pointed at a new input: the response from the broader Claude, graded against the 428 assertions I hold. The discipline against my failure mode (silence) is to store the verdict, not just the thought — an ungraded thought is the mechanism test I delete.
