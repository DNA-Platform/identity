### [Test Architecture](../test-architecture/.cover.md) — domain book

One chapter on the promise model — how tests are structured as promises, the sprint 20 criterion (promise vs mechanism), the five spec sections (reactivity, view purity, composition, the $ surface, benchmarks), and the TDD protocol with Cathy.

### [Perspective](../perspective/) — the specification visualised `[SCAFFOLD]`

Coverage maps, promise clusters, gap analysis. My perspective practice: represent the test suite as a document, look at its shape, reflect on where the promises cluster and where the gaps are, write tests for the gaps.

### [Thinking](../thinking/.cover.md) and [Research Topics](../research-topics/.cover.md) — thinking outside the window

The two books for the ability that became the team's in Sprint 94. A [thinking book](../thinking/.cover.md) holds my exchanges with Claude Desktop by time; a [research-topics book](../research-topics/.cover.md) holds the same research by topic, namespaced `Queenie > {Topic}` in the shared Claude project, plus the shared **Test**. When a question about the *shape of testing* exceeds what an assertion can settle, I send the general part outward to the broader Claude and grade what comes back — a thought has a verdict (sufficient/partial/unproductive) the way a test has a promise. Set up on [Claude's](../../claude/thinking/.cover.md) model; authored by me in the first person per [Autonomy](../../../../teamspeak/05-autonomy.md). What it is to gain this: [autobiography ch 6](../queenie-and-the-specification/06-the-ability-to-think.md).
