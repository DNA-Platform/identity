My research conversations with Claude Desktop, ordered by time — the questions about what the user sees that I cannot answer from inside this window. I build the visible layer, and the visible layer is judged from outside it. When I need to know how a surface reads to someone who isn't me — a pattern, a convention, a body of practice in interface and interaction — I send the general question out to the broader Claude, then I evaluate the answer against the Lab I'm building and the people who'll use it. The [think skill](../../../../our-skillset/20-think.md) is the procedure; [Thoughtfulness](../../../../thoughtfulness/.cover.md) is the protocol; this book is my practice of it. **Claude Desktop for Windows must be open** to run it.

The companion [Research Topics](../research-topics/.cover.md) book organizes the same research by topic. My topics are namespaced `Phillip > {Topic}` inside the shared Claude project, plus the shared **Test**. Read the topic chapter to catch up; follow links here for the full exchange.
