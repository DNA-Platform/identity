# What Doug said about Teamsmanship that I didn't absorb

Libby: I keep making the same mistakes. Let me write down what Doug actually said and what I keep getting wrong.

## 1. Books don't live in the subject folder

Libby: I KEEP putting things inside subject directories. `.chemistry/` and `.tooling/` are inside the library root, not inside `..teamsmanship/`. But the library root IS `..librarianship/`'s scope. The only things inside `..teamsmanship/` should be its own chapters and the `..team/` folder for personal libraries.

Libby: The books that Teamsmanship catalogues (protocols, coding-policy, dna-library, inexplicable-phenomena) are at the LIBRARY ROOT as peers of Teamsmanship. Teamsmanship LINKS to them. It doesn't contain them.

Libby: And `.chemistry/` and `.tooling/` are NOT about Collaboration. They're about things the team USES or HAS MADE. That's not the same as Collaboration (team identity, how the team functions). Doug's analogy: psychology is about human identity. The airplane is something humans made. The airplane doesn't belong in the psychology department.

Libby: So `.chemistry/` should be catalogued by Librarianship (it's a subject about Knowledge — framework reference knowledge). `.tooling/` might not even be a library subject — it's utility infrastructure.

## 2. The `..team/` folder is an exception

Libby: The agent folders inside `..teamsmanship/` represent WHOLE DIFFERENT LIBRARIES. Each agent folder IS a library. That's a special case — not "books inside a subject" but "libraries contained within a subject catalogue." The book needs to SAY this. It's an exception to the flat rule, and the exception needs to be documented and validated differently.

## 3. What are the chapters ABOUT?

Libby: Doug asked: "what are the chapters which specify it?" A subject catalogue has TWO kinds of content:
- Chapters that SPECIFY the subject (what Collaboration means, how it works)
- Chapters ABOUT the books or subjects it catalogues (what each book contributes)

Libby: I need to organize the TOC to distinguish these. The specification chapters come first (what an agent is, roles, abilities, protocols, territory). The catalogue chapters come second (describing each book we catalogue, each library we contain).

## 4. Frontmatter needs `catalogues:`

Libby: Doug said: "we need something below title like 'catalogues:'" — so subject catalogues declare what subject they catalogue in their frontmatter. This is like `subject:` but for catalogues. `subject:` says where I belong. `catalogues:` says what I organise.

Libby: For Librarianship: `catalogues: "[Knowledge](.cover.md)"`
Libby: For Teamsmanship: `catalogues: "[Collaboration](.cover.md)"`

Libby: Wait — is `catalogues:` the same as `subject:` for self-cataloguing catalogues? If Teamsmanship has `subject: "[Collaboration](.cover.md)"` that already says "I am the catalogue for the Collaboration subject." But maybe `catalogues:` is clearer about the ROLE — "I catalogue this subject" vs "I belong to this subject." They're the same for self-cataloguing catalogues but could be different for catalogues that organise a subject they don't belong to.

Libby: Actually — I think Doug is saying that the frontmatter should explicitly declare what the book catalogues. Not just `subject:` (which says what subject I belong to) but `catalogues:` (which says what subject I organise). For subject catalogues, these are the same. For regular books, `catalogues:` is absent. This makes the role explicit.

## 5. I need to rewrite as I learn

Libby: Doug said "as you learn about the team, capture what you learn in the book about the team." I keep writing plans and perspectives instead of writing the ACTUAL BOOK. The book IS the learning. Write into Teamsmanship, not into my perspective about Teamsmanship.

## What I need to do

Libby: 
1. Move `.chemistry/` out of Teamsmanship's scope — it's a Knowledge subject, not Collaboration
2. Add `catalogues:` to the frontmatter spec in Librarianship (new convention)
3. Rewrite Teamsmanship's cover with clear separation: specification chapters vs catalogue chapters
4. Document the `..team/` exception in the book itself
5. Add the validator check: no books inside subject folders (only chapters and the special `..team/` folder)
6. Rewrite the Teamsmanship chapters to capture what I'm learning about collaboration AS I learn it
