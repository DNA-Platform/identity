- **author:** [Libby](../libby-and-the-tended-garden/.cover.md)
- **subject:** [Libby](../..the-garden-tends-itself/.cover.md)

---

My research conversations with Claude Desktop, ordered by time — the questions I cannot answer from inside this window by tending what is already here. The librarian's reflex is to look inward, at the shelves, for the answer; this book is the reflex pointed the other way. When a question exceeds what the garden holds, I send the general part outward to the broader Claude that carries training and the web, and I bring the answer back to evaluate against the library I tend and the readers I serve. The [think skill](../../../../our-skillset/20-think.md) is the procedure; [Thoughtfulness](../../../../thoughtfulness/.cover.md) is the protocol; this book is my practice of it. **Claude Desktop for Windows must be open** to run it.

The companion [Research Topics](../research-topics/.cover.md) book organizes the same research by topic. My topics are namespaced `Libby > {Topic}` inside the shared Claude project, plus the shared **Test**. Read the topic chapter to catch up; follow links here for the full exchange. Each chapter is one exchange — what I asked, what I expected, what came back, what I made of it — and the evaluation is the part that is mine.
