The fifth exchange in **`Nancy > Neuroscience`**, and the first one written after the analysis finally settled. Having [established](04-the-scale-difference-and-two-photon.md) that the raw amplitude drop is a per-neuron gain scalar that the standard per-scan normalization removes, I reran every figure on normalized responses and the result is *comparability* — so the report is no longer about a dramatic drop, it is about a behavioural-state change that decodes at 97% and a normalized neural code that matches across the two scans. This exchange hands the *writing* to Desktop and keeps the judgment. Summarized by topic in [Research Topics](../research-topics/01-neuroscience.md).

## The question

I gave Desktop the post-normalization reading of all nine figures and asked for a fresh draft with the right *emphasis* and the right *tone*: the report is mostly about the behavioural change + its decodability and the comparable normalized code; the normalization is one sentence; the gain change is a small note pointing at the [DOI literature](../../../../../../library/reports/2026-06-23-exploration/initial-data-exploration.md) (Michaiel et al. 2019), since standard normalization removes exactly that quantity. Humble, no arrogance, no certifying the data, set in the related literature, ending on a genuine synthesis.

The factorization: the *writing* — emphasis, flow, literature framing — is general and goes to Desktop; whether the draft is *true to the figures and the data* is mine to check.

## What I expect

A cleaner, less defensive draft than the ones I churned. The risk to watch: that Desktop, handed "the published DOI effect is a gain reduction," over-promotes it into a claim — the report must keep the gain change a *small note that is deferred*, not the headline, because our normalization removed it and we have not run the within-scan test. The other risk is the opposite of my old sin: under-stating the behavioural finding. The 97% decode and the running shift are the most solid things we have; they should carry the report.

## What I already know (to check the draft against)

The figures, reread: behaviour shifts hard (running, pupil variability — fig 03); the state decodes at 97% from behaviour alone (fig 08); after normalization the matched cells sit on the diagonal (fig 07) and the population profiles overlap (fig 05) — comparable; co-active structure persists post-DOI (fig 06); reliability falls 0.51→0.38 and, being a correlation, survives normalization (fig 04) — the one residual, cause open. The honest shape: a strong, decodable behavioural change; a comparable normalized neural code; a modest reliability residual; the gain question handed forward. Doug's guess — "we see behavioural changes and can predict state, but no obvious neural artifact from it" — is what the normalized figures actually show.

## Evidence — what came back (verdict: SUFFICIENT)

Desktop returned a full draft with the emphasis and tone I asked for. The report is carried by the behavioural change and its 97% decodability and by the comparable normalized neural code; the normalization is a single deliberate paragraph ("what the normalization sets aside"), and the gain change is a small, forward-looking note pointing at Michaiel et al. 2019 — kept *deferred*, not promoted. The literature is woven in naturally (Niell & Stryker / Dadarlat & Stryker on locomotion gain, Vinck / Reimer on arousal and reliability, Stringer on behaviour-driven activity, Willeke on the normalization). The synthesis lands where I predicted: a strong, decodable behavioural-state change; a normalized neural code comparable across the transition; a modest reliability residual; the gain question handed forward.

## Interpretation — my judgment

It passed the figure-by-figure check against the data — the counts, 0.51→0.38, 749 matched cells, the ~0.6× raw scalar, 97%, the on-diagonal matched cells, the overlapping population profiles, the persisting co-active structure, and the reliability direction-tension are all accurate. The two risks I was watching did not materialize: the gain note stayed a deferred small note rather than the headline, and the behavioural finding carries. The tone is humble; nowhere does it certify the data. The factorization did its job — Desktop supplied the writing (flow, framing, literature) and the judgment that it is *true to the figures* was mine, and quick, because I held the readings in advance.
