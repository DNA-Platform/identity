# How behaviour is fed — channels, modulator, or the thing we missed

- **author:** [Nancy](../nancy-or-the-weight-of-evidence/.cover.md)
- **date:** 2026-06-25
- **topic:** Nancy > The Twin
- **previous:** [Sourcing the reconstruction method](10-sourcing-the-reconstruction-method.md)
- **status:** concluded (the modulator lean was reversed by the Franke follow-up — see the follow-up section)

---

The sixth exchange of **`Nancy > The Twin`**, and it reopens a door I thought [the audit](07-the-full-pipeline-audit.md) had closed. Doug's framing is exact and it is a discipline check: *the answer is always — what did they already do?* We have a symptom (the readout retinotopy collapses in y, but **only when behaviour is on**), and before I theorize about it I am to go find the **published example** — the actual GitHub config that trains a behaviour-conditioned twin on Reimer-lab data — and read what the papers' **methods and appendices** actually say. Not the prose deep-dive; the code and the supplement. I represent the Reimer lab reproducing Tolias's exact technique, and "we think we match" is not good enough when a config we blessed produces something physically impossible.

## The question

The general part, to Desktop with web search: **how is behaviour fed in the canonical Sinz/Tolias/Reimer V1 twin** — as **input channels** (`add_behavior_as_channels`, our current setup) or as a **multiplicative `MLPModulator` gain** on the readout output, or both — and *which is the published default*? Concrete repo→file→config-key pointers (`sinzlab/sensorium` 2022/2023 notebooks, `nnvision`, `neuralpredictors`, `nnfabrik`, `ecker-lab`, `cajal`, DataJoint pipelines), the exact behaviour-variable set and order, and what Franke 2022 / Lurz 2021 / Sensorium 2022–23 say in their **methods and appendices**. I gave it our full config and the precise symptom. The specific part I keep: does the sourced config match our `condition_loaders`, and is our y-collapse the predicted consequence of a divergence?

## Why now — and why this is not redundant with chapter 07

[The audit](07-the-full-pipeline-audit.md) already resolved the behaviour *crux* from Franke 2022's prose: behaviour is **three channels** (pupil size, change in pupil size, locomotion) **plus a pupil-position shifter**, MEIs at 3rd/97th-percentile states — and it judged our channels+shifter Arm A "canonical, almost exactly." So why ask again? Because that was a **prose audit**, and the prose blessed a config that now produces a **flat-line y-retinotopy** — physically impossible for a 2-D-imaged population ([the measurement-not-the-data lesson](../nancy-or-the-weight-of-evidence/.cover.md)). Either (a) the audit was right and the collapse is a separable numerical pathology, or (b) a detail the prose did not pin — *how* the three channels enter relative to the `grid_mean_predictor`, a modulator we omitted, the CoordConv x/y position channels chapter 07 noted Franke uses and we drop, a normalization step — is the cause. The only way to tell (a) from (b) is to read the **actual example code and the appendices**, which is exactly what a prose deep-dive cannot give. This is the "what did they already do?" check applied to the one config under suspicion.

## What I already know — the context I'll judge the answer against

- **Chapter 07's verdict:** behaviour-as-three-channels + pupil shifter is canonical per Franke 2022; we match; warm-start was the only twin divergence. It also flagged, in passing, that **Franke uses two CoordConv x/y position channels we omit** — filed then as harmless for grayscale, but now a live suspect for a *positional* collapse.
- **Our exact wiring:** `static_loaders(include_behavior=True, include_eye_position=True)` with sensorium default `add_behavior_as_channels=True` → 4-channel input (stim + 3 flat behaviour channels); `pupil_center` → `MLPShifter` (arrives z-scored); `gamma_shifter=0` (default, and the MLP shifter's `regularizer()` is a hard 0); behaviour std-scaled, **not** mean-subtracted (running enters at mean ~4.1, a positive DC on a flat channel); core BatchNorm on.
- **The symptom:** behaviour-on → readout `mu` y-std ~0.004 (flat line); behaviour-off (stimulus-only, no shifter) → clean 2-D retinotopy matching the known-good twin. The cells are genuinely 2-D (two-photon plane, Reimer-cleaned, Tolias MEIs exist), so the collapse is **model/config, not data**.
- **The standing alternative:** `neuralpredictors` has an `MLPModulator` (`x * (elu(MLP(behavior)) + 1)`) — a strictly-positive per-neuron gain on the readout **output**, behaviour never entering the spatial core. CD earlier named this the production-grade way to include a global state signal without spatial competition. Our builder does not use it.

## What I expect (a prediction to measure against)

- **Most likely (60%):** the canonical Sinz/Tolias behaviour-conditioned twin feeds behaviour **as input channels** (our setup is structurally right), and the y-collapse is **not** the published config's fault but a numerical pathology of *our particular* combination — uncentered behaviour DC + an unconstrained shifter co-adapting with the cortical `grid_mean_predictor`. If so the cure is config hygiene (centre behaviour, or constrain/seed the shifter), not a different architecture.
- **Plausible (30%):** there is a **modulator** or a **position-channel** detail in the actual example we dropped — behaviour enters via `MLPModulator` on the output, or the CoordConv x/y channels are load-bearing for the readout geometry — and omitting it is what lets behaviour bleed into the vertical retinotopy. This is the (b) branch and the most valuable outcome.
- **Genuine uncertainty:** whether a *public* example trains behaviour-conditioned on this exact data at all (the Sensorium competition data may ship behaviour but the winning configs may differ), and whether the appendices specify the shifter/modulator config precisely enough to copy.
- **The discipline I am testing myself on:** not to repeat the chapter-07 move of blessing a config from prose. I want a **file and a line**, or an appendix quote — the example, not the description.

## Evidence — what came back (verdict: SUFFICIENT, with one thread left open)

The reframe that matters: **there is no single "canonical" behaviour injection — there are two, split by pipeline, and we copied the one built for a competition, not the one built for MEIs.**

- **Two pipelines, named and sourced.** (a) **Sensorium competition static baseline = behaviour-as-channels** — locomotion, pupil size, instantaneous pupil-change appended to the grayscale image as core input; horizontal+vertical pupil position feed the shifter. This is *exactly* what we built (`mouse_loaders.py` `add_behavior_as_channels=True` + the 1b notebook). A faithful reproduction — of the starter kit. (b) **Production Tolias/Sinz inception-loop pipeline (the one that actually makes MEIs) = a behavioural adaptive-gain MODULATOR** — `x * (elu(MLP(behavior)) + 1)` on the readout output; behaviour **never enters the spatial core**. Cited from **Ding/Tolias 2026 (Nat Neurosci)**: "three-layer convolutional core, single-point readout, a shifter network for eye movements, and a behavioral modulator predicting neuron-specific adaptive gain," Sinz-2018/Walker-2019 lineage. So "the way the lab itself would" *for MEI synthesis* is the modulator; channels is the competition simplification.
- **Repo → file pointers (the file-and-line I demanded).** Channels: `sinzlab/sensorium` → `sensorium/datasets/mouse_loaders.py` (`add_behavior_as_channels`), notebook `1b_model_training_sensorium+`. Modulator: `sinzlab/neuralpredictors` → `layers/modulators/mlp.py` (`MLPModulator`, the `x*(elu(MLP(b))+1)` gain) and `layers/encoders/firing_rate.py`, where the encoder applies `x = self.modulator[data_key](x, behavior=behavior)`. **Caveat:** that file carries a *stale* docstring ("Modulators not implemented atm, 24/06/2021") — the hook is functional (the forward I traced is real) but the **static-twin** modulator is less battle-tested than the dynamic one. The production GRU modulator lives in `nnvision/legacy/.../dynamic_models/` (`build_modulator(..., gamma_modulator, offset)`) and has a **real `gamma_modulator` regularizer** — unlike the MLP shifter's no-op `regularizer()` we found last turn. The static gauss-readout builders (`se_core_full_gauss_readout`) wire **neither** by default; the modulator is added at the encoder/build level. **Walker 2019 (static images + gain modulator) is the published precedent that static+modulator is a real, validated config** — so this is not off-lineage.
- **Variables and a cheap independent bug-source.** Core set: locomotion, pupil, pupil-change; eye position (h+v pupil position) separate, shifter-only — matches our split. ⚠️ **The order of the three channels is set by the behaviour array in the data `.zip` meta, NOT guaranteed by the loader** — verify it from the file rather than trusting the remembered "running/pupil/pupil-deriv." Preprocessing confirms last turn: behaviour `/std`, no mean subtraction (running enters as a large positive DC; BatchNorm neutralizes it); eye z-scored.
- **The papers — and the honest gap.** Ding 2026 says modulator, explicitly. **Franke 2022** — the behaviour-twin paper closest to us and the one [chapter 07](07-the-full-pipeline-audit.md) audited against — states it "extended a recently described model to predict responses jointly from visual input and behaviour" (refs = Walker 2019 + Lurz 2021), cortical 2-D coordinate readout, linear 2→2 + tanh shifter on the pupil centre. **CD could NOT retrieve the exact Franke sentence specifying channels-vs-modulator, and flagged it UNVERIFIED rather than guess** — inferring modulator only from the Walker lineage. A follow-up to fetch the Franke bioRxiv methods (`2021.09.03.458870v2`) and pull the exact sentence is **in flight**.
- **The collapse.** No GitHub issue or published caveat names "behaviour-as-channels destabilises `grid_mean_predictor`." So: **not documented, but mechanistically expected** — the single shared `mu_transform` MLP can zero its low-signal y-output in lockstep once behaviour channels supply an alternative variance sink; an output-gain modulator structurally *cannot* compete with the core for the y-axis. Present as expected, not cited.
- **Other source:** the appendix of "Learning to cluster neuronal function" (arXiv 2506.03293 §B.4) is the clearest secondary description of the exact Sensorium-2022 behaviour wiring; Reimer-lab DataJoint code isn't public.

## Interpretation — vs my prediction

I split 60/30 between "channels is structurally right, the collapse is a numerical pathology of our mix" and "we dropped a modulator/position-channel." **The answer lands on the 30% branch — and sharpens it past what I'd imagined:** it isn't that we omitted a piece of *our* pipeline, it's that there are **two published pipelines** and we faithfully built the *competition* one when the *MEI* one is what we need. My lower-probability hypothesis was the right one; I under-weighted it because chapter 07 had told me channels was canonical — and that is the lesson doubling back on me.

The collapse is now *explained, from the lab's own method*, not theorised: behaviour-as-channels can starve the shared low-variance y-`mu`; the output-gain modulator never touches it. This vindicates both the mechanism we nailed last turn **and** Doug's instinct — "you misunderstood how behaviour was used if the way you did it collapsed retinotopy."

My discipline check — *file-and-line, not prose* — **passed on the modulator side** (`mlp.py`, the `firing_rate.py` hook line: concrete) but **the one point that matters most for us, Franke specifically, came back UNVERIFIED**, and CD was honest about it. That is the exact risk I opened this chapter naming: chapter 07's "clean match to Franke 2022 [channels]" was itself an over-read from prose. So I will *not* now over-read in the other direction and declare "Franke used a modulator" on the Walker-lineage inference — I hold Franke **OPEN** until the methods fetch returns. Same discipline, applied symmetrically.

## Conclusion — a filed thought

**Confirmed, from the lab's own published method:** behaviour-as-channels is the **Sensorium competition baseline**; the **production MEI pipeline** conditions behaviour through a shifter **+ adaptive-gain MODULATOR** that never enters the spatial core. Our y-retinotopy collapse is the **expected consequence** of having reproduced the competition path — behaviour channels competing with the cortical `grid_mean_predictor` for the marginal vertical axis.

**The fix (sourced, published code, no reimplementation):** move behaviour out of the input channels into the `neuralpredictors` **`MLPModulator`** on the readout output, **keep the eye-position shifter**, turn `add_behavior_as_channels` off. Confirm the lockstep mechanism once with the `grid_mean_predictor=None` learned-`mu` diagnostic (our plain-mu test already points this way). Note the static-modulator-hook caveat (stale docstring; less battle-tested than the dynamic GRU) — verify the forward on our stack as we wire it.

**Before committing, close the gap — this is the "exact right answer" Doug demanded:** lock down **Franke 2022 channels-vs-modulator** from the bioRxiv methods (follow-up in flight). Do not bless the modulator from the Walker-lineage *inference* the way chapter 07 blessed channels from *prose*. **And, independent of all of it:** verify the three behaviour channels' **order** from the data `.zip` meta, not the remembered order.

**For the team:** the **stimulus-only twins (arm B)** are untouched by this — clean 2-D retinotopy, unblocked now; behaviour is optional for the locus-of-representation question regardless. The behaviour arm is a **contained, sourced architecture swap** (channels → modulator), a build decision for Doug, not a prerequisite. **And chapter 07's record needs a correction:** its "Franke = behaviour-as-channels" was prose-inferred and is now OPEN — the [research-topic cover](../research-topics/02-the-twin.md) carries that forward.

> **⚠ SUPERSEDED by the follow-up below.** The methods fetch reversed the modulator lean: **Franke 2022 used behaviour-as-channels, not a modulator** — so the fix is **not** the `MLPModulator` but the **CoordConv position channels** Franke pairs with the behaviour channels and that we dropped. The conclusion above stands only as the record of the pre-verification lean.

## Follow-up — Franke pinned, and the lean reversed

The methods fetch came back and **reversed CD's own modulator lean** — which is exactly why I held it open instead of acting on the first answer.

**Verdict: Franke 2022 fed behaviour as INPUT CHANNELS, not a gain modulator.** The decisive line is the Fig 1e architecture (Nature/PMC): *"the model input is two image channels, three behaviour channels and two position channels encoding the x and y pixel position of the input images, followed by a 4-layer convolutional core, a Gaussian readout and a nonlinearity, with read-out positions adjusted using a shifter network."* No behavioural gain modulator anywhere in Franke; the three behaviour variables enter as spatially-broadcast channels to the core, **exactly as we do.** Shifter confirmed: a linear 2→2 + tanh on the pupil centre — our `MLPShifter`. CD's explicit self-correction: "Franke extends Walker 2019, so a modulator is the likely route" was **wrong inference** — the modulator-as-canonical claim holds for the *inception-loop production lineage* (Sinz 2018, Walker 2019, Ding 2026), **not** for Franke 2022, the paper we actually audited against.

**The real deviation is the forgotten step Doug predicted.** Franke's input stack is **2 image + 3 behaviour + 2 CoordConv (x,y) position channels** (ref Liu 2018); ours is **1 image + 3 behaviour + 0 position channels.** The behaviour channels are *not* the deviation — the dropped **CoordConv position channels** are. And we did not find this blind: [chapter 07](07-the-full-pipeline-audit.md) **catalogued it** — "Franke also uses two CoordConv x/y position channels we omit" — and dismissed it as "fine for grayscale single-channel." *That dismissal is the cataloguing failure Doug named at the outset.* The position channels hand the core explicit absolute x,y pixel position — the anchor the readout needs to keep retinotopy from degenerating. Franke ran behaviour-as-channels **with** them and reported no collapse; we ran it **without** them and y collapsed.

**It is coherent against all three observations:** arm B (no behaviour, no position) is fine; arm A (behaviour, no position) collapses; Franke (behaviour, *with* position) is fine. The position channels anchor absolute retinotopy; without them the behaviour channels hand the core flat variance sinks and the shared `mu_transform` MLP is free to zero its low-signal y-output. **Still a hypothesis to test, not a certainty** — CD reversed its own lean once this very exchange, so I hold this as *leading-and-testable*, not settled.

**The discipline is the real win.** The first answer said "switch to the modulator"; I refused to bless it from the Walker-lineage inference — the chapter-07 trap run in reverse — and one follow-up later Franke's own Fig 1e overturned it. Acting on the first answer would have moved us *away* from the paper we audited against. *Get the methods quote before committing* is what caught it; it is the same lesson as the retinotopy collapse itself — do not let a plausible story, or a plausible fix, outrun the verification.

**Revised fix, ranked (most Franke-faithful first):**
1. **Add the two CoordConv (x,y) position channels** (Liu 2018) — reproduces Franke's exact Fig 1e input stack, smallest deviation, directly tests whether the missing position anchor is what let y collapse.
2. **`grid_mean_predictor=None` learned-mu** — one-line diagnostic to confirm the shared-MLP lockstep (our plain-mu test already leans this way).
3. **Behaviour gain modulator** — principled, matches the production inception-loop pipeline, but a **deviation from Franke specifically** — held in reserve.

Independent of all of it: **verify the 3 behaviour channels' order** from the data `.zip` meta, not the remembered order.

**For the team (corrected):** this corrects both chapter 11's first conclusion (which leaned modulator) and chapter 07 (whose "Franke = behaviour-as-channels, clean match" was right about the channels but missed that the match was *incomplete* — we dropped the position channels Franke pairs them with). Stimulus-only **arm B** is untouched and unblocked. The behaviour arm now has a **concrete, sourced, minimal-deviation fix to test** — add the CoordConv channels — and CD offered the drop-in (the Liu-2018 x,y channels appended to the input, image + behaviour + position as Franke's Fig 1e). **Doug's call** whether to wire and test it, or to leave behaviour out entirely. The channels-vs-modulator question is now **closed: Franke = channels**, and the collapse is traced to a specific, testable, catalogued-and-dismissed omission.
