My topics are namespaced `Nancy > {Topic}` in the shared Claude project, plus the shared **Test** thread that Claude catalogues; the topic switches *within* a Desktop conversation rather than spawning a new chat. Read a topic chapter to catch up on a thread; follow links here for the full exchange.

Chapters link through `previous:` fields for conversation chains. The cover catalogues every exchange with the conversation it happened in and its state.

## Conversations

2. [A codebase that outlives the project](02-a-codebase-that-outlives-the-project.md) — 2026-06-23, continuing the same `Nancy > Neuroscience` conversation. How to arrange a research codebase to outlive one project and accrue many analyses, each bound to its prose. Desktop named our pure-core/effects-at-edges design (functional-core/imperative-shell, hexagonal) and gave six patterns; the two that land on my lens are *the stats/confound layer as the most-frozen, most-property-tested code* (my gate gets teeth) and *the core/volatile boundary as a social contract* (editing the core to make a hypothesis land is the elegant-assumption trap). Resolved the package fork to an installable src-layout monorepo; first `analyses/` module still open to Doug.
3. [Writing the exploration well](03-writing-the-exploration-well.md) — 2026-06-23, same `Nancy > Neuroscience` conversation. A turn from *what to build* to *how to communicate*: I handed Desktop the whole [Initial Data Exploration](../../../../../../library/reports/2026-06-23-exploration/initial-data-exploration.md) report for a prose pass — more fluid, more expert, same length, lose no detail — and signed off only after checking the rewrite against the analysis. Every caveat (single-split reliability, geometric match, per-session ordering, arbitrary units) and the no-causal-claim spine survived; the value was the *checking*, which is mine alone. Two tooling notes filed: the `/think` CLI caps prompts near 8 KB (a file-input helper works around it), and Desktop defaults to a canvas for long outputs — ask for inline raw text.
4. [The scale difference, and whether two-photon makes it](04-the-scale-difference-and-two-photon.md) — 2026-06-23, `Nancy > Neuroscience`. The matched-cell ~48% drop is a per-neuron *multiplicative scalar* (mean and s.d. fall in lockstep) that the dataset's per-session standardization erases — and is *non-identifiable* from this comparison between genuine V1 gain modulation (plausible for a 5-HT2A agonist) and cross-session measurement scale, because standardizing removes the very axis the effect lives on. Method lesson: verify the pipeline's own normalization before any cross-session magnitude claim; erasure-by-standardization is diagnostic, not exculpatory.
5. [Writing it up after normalization](05-writing-it-up-after-normalization.md) — 2026-06-23, `Nancy > Neuroscience`. After reranning every figure on normalized responses (which gives comparability), I handed the *writing* to Desktop and kept the judgment. The report is now carried by the behavioural-state change and its 97% decodability and by the comparable normalized code; the normalization is one paragraph and the gain change a deferred note pointing at Michaiel et al. 2019. Passed the figure-by-figure fidelity check; the gain note stayed deferred, the behavioural finding carried.
6. [The publication twin, and the metric mismatch](06-the-twin-and-the-metric-mismatch.md) — 2026-06-23, opening the new `Nancy > The Twin` conversation. Our single-seed twin hits ~0.26 vs the paper's 0.61 — but our core (Lurz-2021 `stacked_core_full_gauss_readout`) and trainer (`standard_trainer`) *are* the publication recipe verbatim; the gap is mostly a **metric mismatch** (single-trial vs correlation-to-average), not a model gap. No-retrain fix: noise-ceiling-normalized correlation-to-average on the repeated test images. The keeper meta-lesson: same trap as MEI-as-reconstruction — check two numbers are the same *kind* of number before reading their difference as a deficiency.
7. [The full-pipeline audit](07-the-full-pipeline-audit.md) — 2026-06-24, `Nancy > The Twin`. The whole pipeline audited against the canonical Reimer/Tolias papers as the last check on Sprint 5 (verdict SUFFICIENT). Mostly canonical — normalization and twin verbatim, the MEI gradient recipe an exact `nnvision` match, and the behaviour/shifter/fixed-state design a clean match to Franke 2022, which resolved the crux: MEIs are synthesized at **3rd/97th-percentile quiet/active** states (Arm A validated). Four plan changes: label the metric honestly (repo reports **FEVE**, ours is Schoppe **CC_norm**); **train post cold** (demote warm-start); document **p=2 as our contrast-constraint**, not the nnvision op; add the **temporal-drift null** (highest-priority DOI control). I predicted the behaviour crux and the warm-start divergence; I was wrong that the gradient constants need rescaling (they're verbatim — the scale-sensitivity lives in the p=2 norm budget); and the same-kind-of-number trap bit again at the metric-*label* level.
8. [Gamma — fixed constants or fit per dataset?](08-gamma-fixed-or-fit.md) — 2026-06-24, `Nancy > The Twin`. Do the Sensorium/Tolias twins use fixed `gamma_input`/`gamma_readout` or fit them per dataset? Gated training the four twins. Dispositive (verdict SUFFICIENT, source-traced): **both at different layers** — the *method* (Lurz 2021 / nnfabrik Bayesian-Ax) fits the gammas; the *baseline* freezes one fit (6.3831/0.0076, origin Lurz-2020, proven a fit by the alternate 46.402/0.0207 in the same file) and reuses it across all seven mice. The big correction: our framing was **backwards** — 15.5/4 are the generic library placeholders, our 6.3831/0.0076 ARE the canonical baseline (restoring, not overriding). I nailed share-one-gamma-across-all-four and the search-output tell. Plan: one ~20–30-trial gamma search on Control+behaviour, single seed, validation-correlation objective → near the published values use them for all four, far use the fitted set for all four; **freeze one set, never fit per-condition**; lr overrides fixed/low-stakes.
9. [By-eye verification, and a code review](09-by-eye-verification-and-a-code-review.md) — 2026-06-24, `Nancy > The Twin`. Doug's push that trusting a number isn't verification — *look at the model.* I sent CD the by-eye-panel question and the actual code. The panel I largely predicted (filters / retinotopy / MEIs / predicted-vs-measured / distributions / shifter ablation, each with its broken signature); the **code review caught a CRITICAL bug I missed** — `get_fev`'s 0.15 threshold drops a *different* cell set pre vs post, inflating post FEVE by *selection*, which is why corr-to-average and FEVE pointed opposite ways. The same-kind-of-number trap a third time, now at the population level. Pre-flight fixes filed: fixed-neuron-set discipline, the one canonical matched-pair index, an arm-independent ceiling, the shifter ablation.
10. [Sourcing the reconstruction method](10-sourcing-the-reconstruction-method.md) — 2026-06-24, `Nancy > The Twin`. The *other* essential computation. We had only a prose recipe for the predicted images, and the code is NOT bundled in our tools the way the MEI ops were (searched `nnvision`/`mei`/site-packages — nothing). Settled (verdict SUFFICIENT, and a reframe): the method is the lab's **metamer synthesis** (Cobos et al. 2022, *neural metamers*, bioRxiv 2022.12.09.519708) — blank → SGD on the image → MSE to repeat-averaged responses over ALL cells, constant σ=2.5 gradient blur, step-size+σ tuned by SSIM. The crux: **no public code exists** (internal DataJoint) and the Methods fully specify it — so here, unlike the MEIs, faithful **reimplementation is the principled path**, which Doug blessed; the metamer module is written and tested (re-evokes the recorded response at 0.79, pixel-corr 0.09 — response-faithful, not pixel-faithful). The 0.61 is the encoding CC_norm; the metamer ceiling is 0.908, which DOI moves.
11. [How behaviour is fed — channels, modulator, or the thing we missed](11-how-behaviour-is-fed.md) — 2026-06-25, `Nancy > The Twin`. A behaviour-on retinotopy collapse (readout y-`mu` goes flat) sent me back to the published source for a file-and-line, not prose. The reframe: there is **no single canonical behaviour injection — there are two pipelines**, and we faithfully built the *competition* one (`add_behavior_as_channels`, sensorium 1b) when the pipeline that actually makes MEIs uses a multiplicative **`MLPModulator`** adaptive gain on the readout output (Ding/Tolias 2026; `neuralpredictors/layers/modulators/mlp.py` + the `firing_rate.py` hook). Behaviour-as-channels can starve the shared low-variance y-`mu`; an output-gain modulator structurally cannot — so the collapse is explained from the lab's own method, vindicating Doug's "you misunderstood how behaviour was used." My 30% branch was right; I'd under-weighted it because chapter 07 blessed channels from prose. Discipline check passed on the modulator (concrete file/line) but **Franke-specifically came back UNVERIFIED** — held OPEN pending the bioRxiv methods fetch, the over-reading-from-prose risk applied symmetrically.
12. [The brutal audit, and the gain-modulator turn](12-the-brutal-audit-and-the-gain-modulator-turn.md) — 2026-06-27, `Nancy > The Twin`. A whole-pipeline audit (1200-line compilation → CD), enabled by fixing the `/think` artifact capture so his *code* returns as clean files. **The y-collapse is resolved:** it's the **shared cortex `grid_mean_predictor` MLP** zeroing its marginal-y row under behaviour-as-channels (feature map 28×56 — my ~10×38 was wrong, hidden layers pad); **data verified clean in source** (cortex grid = `cell_motor_coordinates[:,:2]`, image not transposed, y-input real 132 µm — Doug's "wrong level" check confirmed, not a misread column). CD **changed its call on the data check** (rigour, not convenience): build both, **prefer the gain-modulator** (keeps the canonical predictor, IS the production-MEI behaviour mechanism, retinotopy survives by construction, the gain = our DOI effect) / **learned-μ confirmed fallback**, by an empirical y-spread+FEVE-parity rule. Plus 4 real bugs: `get_fev` **filters** (the fixed-set discipline silently doesn't run — the ch-09 artifact, still live), the muddiness fix never wired into the runner (contrast constraint, not clip), metamer arm-B-only, arm-A MEI state trial-0 not percentile. Standing lesson (Doug): verify the **data-reading level** in source before deviating the architecture.
