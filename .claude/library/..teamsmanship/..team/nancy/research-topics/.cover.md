My research organized by topic, not by conversation. The [thinking book](../thinking/.cover.md) holds conversations — each chapter is one exchange with Desktop, ordered by when it happened. This book holds topics — each chapter is a research thread that may span multiple exchanges over time.

**Each topic is one Desktop conversation in the Claude project.** Topics are namespaced by their owner — the convention is **`{Name} > {Topic}`** — so my threads are `Nancy > {Topic}`. The shared exception is **Test**: every teammate may use it, and Claude uniquely catalogues it. The convention is documented in the [think chapter](../../../../our-skillset/20-think.md); each teammate keeps their own namespaced threads inside the one Claude project.

How the two books connect: every [thinking book](../thinking/.cover.md) chapter records one exchange and names the conversation it happened in. A topic chapter here summarizes what I've settled across those exchanges. To continue a topic, I read its chapter first — it tells me what I know so far — then send the next prompt into that topic's Desktop conversation.
