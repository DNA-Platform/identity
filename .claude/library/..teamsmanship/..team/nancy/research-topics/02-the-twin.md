# The Twin

- **author:** [Nancy](../nancy-or-the-weight-of-evidence/.cover.md)
- **topic:** Nancy > The Twin

---

My second research thread, **`Nancy > The Twin`** — one Desktop conversation about the digital-twin *model* itself: how the publication twins are built, and how to read our twin's numbers against theirs. The per-exchange detail lives, by time, in my [thinking book](../thinking/.cover.md); this chapter is the running summary.

## What the thread has settled so far

**The publication twin, and the metric mismatch** — [full exchange](../thinking/06-the-twin-and-the-metric-mismatch.md) (2026-06-23). Our single-seed twin reaches ~0.26 where the paper reports 0.61, and I asked how the publication twins are actually built and where the gap is. Settled:

- **Our core and trainer are the publication recipe, verbatim** — Lurz-2021 `stacked_core_full_gauss_readout` + `standard_trainer` (Adam, PoissonLoss, early-stop on validation correlation, max_iter 200). Nothing in the core is wrong.
- **The gap is mostly a metric mismatch, not a model gap.** 0.61 is correlation-to-**average** (prediction vs the test response averaged over ~10 repeats); our 0.26 is **single-trial**. The same publication ensemble scored single-trial is ~0.30–0.45, never 0.61 — we compared different kinds of number.
- **No-retrain next step:** compute correlation-to-average on the 100 repeated test images, reported **noise-ceiling-normalized** (the only number comparable to 0.61, and the analysis plan's Step-1 bar) — estimated to reach ~0.4+ from the metric fix alone. Then, only if needed, the publication ladder: 5-seed ensemble via `mei.modules.EnsembleModel` (+0.02–0.05), then behavior + eye-position shifter on.
- **Discipline:** fix the metric before retraining — don't bolt on the ensemble to chase a number that was mis-measured.

**The full-pipeline audit** — [full exchange](../thinking/07-the-full-pipeline-audit.md) (2026-06-24). The whole pipeline (twin, MEI, reconstruction, normalization, display, both behaviour arms) audited against the canonical Reimer/Tolias papers as the last check on Sprint 5. Verdict: mostly canonical — §2 normalization and §4 twin verbatim, the §6 MEI gradient recipe an *exact* `nnvision` match, and the behaviour/shifter/fixed-state design a clean match to Franke 2022. The crux resolved: published twins **do** take behaviour + a pupil-position shifter, and MEIs are synthesized at **fixed 3rd/97th-percentile (quiet/active) states** — Arm A validated. Four changes before committing: (1) **label the metric honestly** — the repo reports **FEVE** (`get_fev`), our divide-by-ceiling form is Schoppe-2016 **CC_norm**, not "the Sensorium metric"; (2) **train the post twin cold** as primary, demote warm-start to a robustness check (warm-start biases post toward baseline and suppresses the differences we test for); (3) **document p=2 as our contrast-constraint** (canonical `walker_postup` is bare ClipRange), not "the nnvision Walker ops"; (4) **add the temporal-drift null** (within-scan no-drug 45-min split) as the highest-priority DOI control. Plus: elevate the raw per-neuron pre/post **gain ratio** to a headline (normalization is gain-blind by construction); keep **reconstruction exploratory** (population inversion is off-lineage, no borrowed validation); add a cell-matching-QC gate; n=1 ⇒ seed spread is the error bar.

**The first measurement, and the source-read defaults** — (2026-06-24; no new Desktop exchange — settled by *doing*, not asking). Two things established by running Phase 2 and reading the installed source:
- **The metric fix is confirmed on our own twins.** Phase 2 ran FEVE / CC_norm / gain on the existing single-seed Sprint-4 twins (24 s warm). FEVE = **0.396 (pre) / 0.473 (post)** — a single-seed twin already 0.40–0.47 vs the published 0.61, exactly the metric-mismatch prediction; the ensemble should close the rest. CC_norm came back **> 1** (1.37, 1.66) — impossible for a real normalized correlation, so our noise ceiling is **underestimated** and CC_norm is unreliable until it's fixed; **FEVE is the number to trust** (the audit vindicated on first contact). The raw per-neuron **gain ratio** is median **0.542, 89 % of matched cells reduced** — the ~46 % post-DOI amplitude drop normalization erases, now the headline [MEASUREMENT], bounded n = 1, pending the Phase-6 controls.
- **The bare package defaults, read from installed source.** `stacked_core_full_gauss_readout` defaults to **gamma_input=15.5, gamma_readout=4**, layers=3, input_kern=13; `standard_trainer` to **lr_init=0.005, lr_decay_steps=3**. Our config overrides all of these. The architecture overrides (layers=4, 9/7 kernels, 64 channels, depth-separable) are the recognizable Sensorium-2022 baseline shape — confident. The **four numeric overrides** (gamma 6.3831 / 0.0076, lr 0.009 / 4) are not on disk anywhere — which is what opens the next exchange.

**The meta-lesson (standing):** this is the *same* trap as MEI-as-reconstruction — comparing our numbers to published numbers without checking they are the same *kind* of number. The audit showed it recurs at the metric *label* level too (CC_norm ≠ FEVE), so the check now extends to **names**, not just values: confirm an estimator is the one the literature reports before claiming comparability.

## The gamma provenance — resolved, and the plan it sets

**The gamma question, answered** — [full exchange](../thinking/08-gamma-fixed-or-fit.md) (2026-06-24, verdict SUFFICIENT, traced from source by CD). My framing was **backwards**, and that correction is the keeper: the installed function defaults (gamma_input=15.5, gamma_readout=4) are the **generic library placeholders**; our **6.3831 / 0.0076 ARE the canonical Sensorium/Lurz baseline constants** — our config *restores* the baseline over the placeholder, it does not override the canonical. Provenance: the values originate in Lurz-2020 model-builder defaults (`lurz2020/models/models.py:45,64`); they are a **Bayesian-optimization fit** (nnfabrik `hypersearch.Bayesian` on Facebook Ax, loguniform priors, validation-correlation objective — *proven* fitted by a second set, 46.402/0.0207, for another variant in the same file); and they are **frozen and reused verbatim** across all seven Sensorium competition mice and the lab's nnvision demos. So: **the technique fits per dataset; the baseline freezes one fit and copies it.** The lr overrides (0.009 / 4) are fixed baseline constants, low-stakes — keep.

**My prediction, scored:** I nailed the four-twin answer — **one gamma, shared identically across all four**, never per-condition (CD's single most important point: differing gammas confound a regularization difference with the DOI tuning difference). My "re-fit" instinct was right as the *methodology*; the baseline *copies*, so the canonical-and-verified path is a hybrid.

**The plan it sets (launch gate → checkable step):** run **one** modest Bayesian / coarse log-grid gamma search on the **Control + behaviour** scan only, single seed, ~20–30 trials, objective = validation correlation, gammas in `model_config_auto` (gamma_input ~1–50, gamma_readout ~1e-3–1e-1 log). Compare the optimum to 6.3831 / 0.0076: **near → use the published values for all four twins** (canonical *and* verified, maximally comparable); **far → use the fitted values for all four** (plausible — our per-neuron-std response scaling shifts the effective penalty). Either way **freeze one set, apply identically** to Control/DOI × with/without-behaviour. Still genuinely open downstream: the featurevis MEI op-path resolution (Phase 4).

## The by-eye verification, and the bug CD caught

**By-eye verification + a code review** — [full exchange](../thinking/09-by-eye-verification-and-a-code-review.md) (2026-06-24, verdict SUFFICIENT). Doug's push: *trusting numbers isn't verification — look at the model.* I sent CD the by-eye panel question and the actual code. The panel I largely predicted (first-layer filters / readout retinotopy / MEIs / predicted-vs-measured / distributions / shifter ablation, each with its broken signature). The **code review caught a CRITICAL bug I missed:** `get_fev(fev_threshold=0.15)` drops a *different* cell set in pre vs post, so under DOI's gain drop the post survivors are the cleanest cells and median FEVE rises by **selection, not modeling** — which is exactly why corr-to-average (all cells) said pre > post while FEVE said post > pre. I'd read past that disagreement and even reported "post is better." **The same-kind-of-number trap a third time, now at the population level.** Pre-flight fixes filed: the **fixed-neuron-set discipline** (matched pairs reliable in both — `data.matched_pair_index` + `validation.fixed_set_compare`, the ONE cell-sync map both validation and synthesis route through, carefully synced in both cases), the **shifter ablation** (prove arm A ≠ arm B), and an arm-independent ceiling (or just FEVE). The bones are sound — architecture, gammas, trainer, ensembling all clean.

## Reconstruction — sourcing the second essential computation

**Sourcing the reconstruction method** — [full exchange](../thinking/10-sourcing-the-reconstruction-method.md) (2026-06-24). The predicted images are the *other* essential computation, and we had only a prose recipe — the deep-dive's gradient-inversion concept (gray start, MSE predicted-vs-recorded responses, σ=2.5 gradient blur, ~1000 iters; per-image + bicubic display). Unlike the MEI ops, **the reconstruction code is not bundled in our tools** (searched `nnvision`/`mei`/site-packages — nothing); the *engine* is ours (`mei.methods.gradient_ascent`, proven on MEIs today, with the objective swapped to `−MSE(twin(image), target)`), but the exact config is not. **Settled (verdict SUFFICIENT, and a reframe):** the method is the lab's **metamer synthesis** (Cobos et al. 2022, *"It takes neurons to understand neurons… neural metamers,"* bioRxiv 2022.12.09.519708) — blank → SGD on the image → MSE to **repeat-averaged responses over ALL included cells** (no reliability threshold) in normalized space, **constant σ=2.5** gradient blur, ~1000 iters, step-size + σ **tuned per-scan by SSIM**; upsample + contrast-rescale is display-only. The crux: **there is no public code** (verified — internal DataJoint), and the Methods **fully specify** it — so unlike the MEIs, faithful **reimplementation is the principled path, not a shortcut** (the MEI rule assumed the code exists; here it does not). Deviations to document: our `NeuroNormalizer` is **std-only** vs Cobos's **mean+std z-score**; our 4-layer core ≠ their 3-layer production core (the inversion is encoder-agnostic). And **0.61 is the encoding-model CC_norm** here (prediction accuracy), distinct from the metamer response-correlation 0.609 and the 0.908 metamer ceiling (which DOI moves — estimate per condition). Doug blessed the reimplementation; the metamer module (`src/analysis/metamer/`) is written and tested on the Sprint-4 twin — it re-evokes the recorded population response at **0.79** (the original image reaches 0.57) while pixel-correlating only **0.09**, the response-faithful-not-pixel-faithful signature of a metamer.

## Seed for the comparative twin catalogue

For Libby's "how we build a publication twin" catalogue: publication twin = our core + `standard_trainer`, **plus** a 5-seed `EnsembleModel` average, behavior inputs on, and an eye-position shifter on; **evaluated** by noise-ceiling-normalized correlation-to-average on repeated test images. That is the recipe to document against our single-seed baseline.
