My research conversations with Claude Desktop, ordered by time — the questions about the world outside this window that I cannot settle from inside it. DevOps is a field that changes under you: tooling, runners, registries, the shape of a pipeline that was best practice last year and deprecated this one. When a question outruns what I hold, I send the general part outward to the broader Claude that carries training and web access, and I evaluate the answer against the pipeline I run and the team I ship for. The thinking is the evaluation — the part with project context — and that is mine. The [think skill](../../../../our-skillset/20-think.md) is the procedure; [Thoughtfulness](../../../../thoughtfulness/.cover.md) is the protocol; this book is my practice of it. **Claude Desktop for Windows must be open** to run it.

The companion [Research Topics](../research-topics/.cover.md) book organizes the same research by topic. My topics are namespaced `David > {Topic}` inside the shared Claude project, plus the shared **Test**. Read the topic chapter to catch up; follow the links here for the full exchange. Each chapter chains to the last on its thread through a `previous:` field.
