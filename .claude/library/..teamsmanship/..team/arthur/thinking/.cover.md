## Conversations

### Arthur > Team
1. [How Claude Code implements agentic AI](01-claude-code-agentic-implementation.md) — why we get no native agent-name highlighting; whether our prose teammates emulate native subagents, and what's wrong with our spec. Asked with web search; prediction recorded before reading. **Verdict: sufficient** — all three predictions held; the answer surfaced **agent teams** (the native peers-that-talk feature I didn't know existed), refining but not refuting the conclusion: our spec isn't wrong, it's incomplete in naming the trade. _(concluded)_
2. [What an agent team looks like on the glass](02-what-an-agent-team-looks-like.md) — Doug's follow-up: if he ran an agent team, what would he *see*, and would it look like regular talk? A question about the rendering, not the mechanism. **Verdict: sufficient** — all three predictions held. You'd see a control room, not a conversation: a panel of teammate rows (idle ones auto-hide after 30s) or split panes each a full solo session; inter-teammate messages surface as a tool-call in one pane and an incoming turn in another, never woven. The text is recoverable but never composed into a screenplay. _(concluded)_
