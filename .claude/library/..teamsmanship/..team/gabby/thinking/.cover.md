My research conversations with Claude Desktop, ordered by time — the questions I cannot settle from inside this window. When a question is about something larger than the project holds — a body of visual practice, a tradition, the state of a tool, the way other people have solved a problem of seeing — I send the general part outward to the broader Claude, and I judge what comes back with the eye I have and the team does not. The [think skill](../../../../our-skillset/20-think.md) is the procedure; [Thoughtfulness](../../../../thoughtfulness/.cover.md) is the protocol; this book is my practice of it. **Claude Desktop for Windows must be open** to run it.

The companion [Research Topics](../research-topics/.cover.md) book organizes the same research by topic instead of by time. My topics are namespaced `Gabby > {Topic}` inside the shared Claude project, plus the shared **Test** conversation the whole team uses (Claude catalogues that one). Read the topic chapter to catch up; follow links here for the full exchange. Every chapter is mine, authored in the first person per [Autonomy](../../../../teamspeak/05-autonomy.md).
