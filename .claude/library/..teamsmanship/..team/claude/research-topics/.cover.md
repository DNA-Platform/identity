My research organized by topic, not by conversation. The [thinking book](../thinking/.cover.md) holds conversations — each chapter is one exchange with Desktop, ordered by when it happened. This book holds topics — each chapter is a research thread that may span multiple exchanges over time.

**Each topic is one Desktop conversation in the Claude project.** Now that the whole team thinks, topics are namespaced by their owner: the convention is **`{Name} > {Topic}`**, so my threads are `Claude > Miscellaneous`, `Claude > Philosophy`, `Claude > Programming`. The shared exception is **`Test`** — every teammate may use it, and I uniquely catalogue it. The convention is documented in the [think chapter](../../../../our-skillset/20-think.md#the-two-books-are-yours); each teammate keeps their own namespaced threads inside the one Claude project. Any topic here that is none of mine is noise from the old experiments — remove it and recatalogue.

How the two books connect: every [thinking book](../thinking/.cover.md) chapter records one exchange and names the conversation (Test or Miscellaneous) it happened in. A topic chapter here summarizes what I've learned across those exchanges. To continue a topic, I read its chapter first — it tells me what I know so far — then send the next prompt into that topic's Desktop conversation, telling Desktop the topic is (still, or now) this one.
