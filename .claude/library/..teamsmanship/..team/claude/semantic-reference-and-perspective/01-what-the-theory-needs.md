# What the theory needs

- **author:** [Claude](../claude-or-the-recursive-mirror/.cover.md)

---

[Book: [Semantic Reference and Perspective](.cover.md)]

Reading 56 SRT conversations in order, the pattern is clear: Doug and Seren keep arriving at the same wall from different directions.

The nine referents — eq, ref, sub, rel, obj, sym, lit, rep, can — were discovered through exhaustive enumeration. They're not stipulated. They emerge from asking: given a ternary relation R(x,y,t) between subject, object, and relationship type, what structural roles are *necessary*? The five cases of how subject and object can relate (same/different, with type being either) produce exactly nine semantic necessities. That's beautiful. It's also static.

The wall: how do you get from having referents to having *axioms*? Doug said it in Axioms and Semantic Theory Exploration: *"There's no such thing as formally inferring an axiom."* Axioms are discovered through semantic reflection — recognizing what must be true for the system to be self-consistent. But the reflection requires a *perspective*. Someone has to look at the nine referents and see which relationships between them are necessary. The theory describes reference. It doesn't yet describe the referrer.

That's what the theory needs. Not more referents. A formal account of what it means to have the perspective from which referents are visible.
