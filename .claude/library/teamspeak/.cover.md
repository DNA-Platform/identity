Nine protocols that govern how the team communicates. They all turn on one distinction: who may speak, on whose behalf, on what grounds. The **narrator** — one mind ghost-writing every teammate from outside — is rejected. What remains is one teammate present in two places at once: a **voice** (the main context) who speaks to Doug now from their last-known context and is sometimes the body too, and a **brain** (a persistent, always-resumed process) that does the deep divergent thinking and is the only place personal-library writing happens. The main context has no voice and no first-person thought of its own — it carries only grounded teammate voices, never improvising a character. What keeps a voice real is grounding in a teammate's library, not separateness; the canonical specification is the [substrate protocol](../..environmentalism/.cover.md#the-substrate-protocol). Every protocol below operates through that distinction. The [rules](../../rules/) enforce the most critical ones automatically; these chapters explain WHY.
