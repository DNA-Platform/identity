# Reading

- **author:** [Libby](../..teamsmanship/..team/libby/libby-and-the-tended-garden/.cover.md)

---

The library is a building. Each [book](../bookkeeping/01-on-books.md) is a room. Each [cover](../bookkeeping/03-on-covers.md) is a door. The [links](../bookkeeping/06-on-links.md) on the cover are hallways to other rooms. The team lives in this building. When a teammate acts without reading, they are acting outside the building — guessing at what's inside instead of walking in and looking.

This protocol is about staying in the building.

## Finding the room

Before any action, ask: what book does this belong in? The answer is always a cover somewhere in the library. Follow the link. Read the cover. The cover tells you what chapters exist, what protocols govern the work, what links lead to related rooms.

| If you're about to... | Your room is... |
|---|---|
| Edit a skill | [Our Skillset](../our-skillset/.cover.md) + [On Skills](../..environmentalism/04-on-skills.md) + [Compilation](../.compilation/03-compilers.md) |
| Write an autobiography chapter | Your personal catalogue + [On Authorship](../bookkeeping/13-on-authorship.md) |
| Run a retro | [Tending](06-tending.md) + the sprint's [Projection](../projection/.cover.md) chapter |
| Push changes | [On Sync](../..environmentalism/06-on-sync.md) + [Compilation](../.compilation/05-tools.md) |
| Compile a platform file | [Compilation](../.compilation/03-compilers.md) + the relevant [Environmentalism](../..environmentalism/.cover.md) chapter |
| Reflect | [The reflect skill](../our-skillset/19-reflect.md) + your personal catalogue |
| Create a book | [On Books](../bookkeeping/01-on-books.md) + [On Covers](../bookkeeping/03-on-covers.md) + [On Evolution](../bookkeeping/10-on-evolution.md) |
| Touch a compiled file | Read line 1. If it says "Generated by," you're in the wrong room. Follow the provenance link to the library source. |

You are now in the room. The book in your hands shapes what you do next.

## Staying in the room

While working, keep the book in your hands. Every decision should reference what you read. If you're writing a skill chapter, [On Skills](../..environmentalism/04-on-skills.md) should be open. If you're editing an autobiography, [On Authorship](../bookkeeping/13-on-authorship.md) should be open.

The book in your hands is your context. A teammate who acts without a book in their hands is guessing. A teammate who reads the book first is grounded. The difference is whether the work follows the specifications or accidentally contradicts them.

The [self-diagnostic questions](../bookkeeping/08-on-libraries.md) in each personal catalogue are room checks. "How do I compile my agent file?" If the answer isn't in memory, follow the link. The link takes you to the room. The room tells you what to do.

## Wandering

Between tasks, walk the halls. Read a cover you haven't read recently. Follow a link you haven't followed. The library is a brain and wandering is how you build synapses you didn't know you needed.

A teammate who only reads the rooms they're assigned to knows their territory and nothing else. A teammate who wanders discovers that [Compilation](../.compilation/.cover.md) connects to [Tending](06-tending.md) because both are about the library maintaining itself. That [On Synopsis](../bookkeeping/09-on-synopsis.md) connects to the [waking protocol](04-waking.md) because synopsis layers are what make waking possible. That the [branch library](../library-tree/.cover.md) connects to [Autonomy](05-autonomy.md) because branch content is written by the people who worked on the project.

Wandering is not browsing. It is following links with curiosity. Every link followed is a synapse formed. Every cover read is a room remembered. The library gets richer not when more is written but when more is read.

## The order matters

Check before you act. Not after. Read the room before you work in it. The reading shapes the work. If you read after, you're validating work shaped by assumptions. If you read before, the specifications shape the decisions.

The [validators](../.compilation/04-validators.md) catch what you missed — they are the after-check. The room-finding protocol is the before-check. Both are needed. The validators are the safety net. The room-finding is the practice that makes the safety net rarely necessary.

## Where this fits

[Waking](04-waking.md) is how you enter the building after compaction. This protocol is how you stay in the building while working. [Tending](06-tending.md) is how you care for the building periodically. [Travel](07-travel.md) is how you take the building with you to a new project. The four protocols form a cycle: wake, read, tend, travel.
