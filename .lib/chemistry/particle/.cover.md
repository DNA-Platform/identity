# Particle

- **author:** [Cathy](../../../../.claude/library/..teamsmanship/..team/cathy/cathy-and-the-reactive-canvas/.cover.md)
- **subject:** [$Chemistry](../..representivity/.cover.md)

---

I am the foundation. Every chemical, every atom, every particularized carrier begins here ‚Äî in the `$Particle` class that gives the framework its ground. I describe the primitive: the identity fields that make each particle uniquely addressable, the lifecycle that orders its phases, the lift function that creates per-mount derivatives, and the view contract that every subclass fulfills. A reader who finishes this book understands what a particle IS before they encounter what a particle can DO in composition. The class is 290 lines. The concepts it carries are the load-bearing floor of everything above.

## Chapters

1. [Identity](01-identity.md) ‚Äî the three identity fields (`$cid$`, `$symbol$`, `$type$`), the `isParticle` marker, and the static symbol-creation machinery. A reader either understands all of it or none of it.
2. [Lifecycle](02-lifecycle.md) ‚Äî six phases (`setup`, `mount`, `render`, `layout`, `effect`, `unmount`), the `next(phase)` progression, `$resolve` propagation up the prototype chain, and the queued-resolver mechanism.
3. [Particularization](03-particularization.md) ‚Äî the constructor's second mode. `new $Particle(particular)` lifts methods onto an existing object, stamps the marker, reparents. The carrier preserves `instanceof`. Self-contained; depends on identity but nothing else.
4. [Lift](04-lift.md) ‚Äî per-mount-site derivation via `Object.create()`. Two parents, the first-mount path, the re-entry path, the render hooks, and the cleanup path. The hooks ARE the derivation flow as React sees it.
5. [Render Filters](05-render-filters.md) ‚Äî the cross-cutting interception chain. `$show`/`$hide`, `registerFilter()`, filter ordering where first non-undefined wins. Tied to lift but conceptually distinct ‚Äî a reader can need filters without re-reading lift.
6. [View](06-view.md) ‚Äî `view()`, `$apply`, augmentation, `$rendering$`, `$viewCache$`. The rendering boundary. Subclass authors override `view()` and the cache mechanism deserves explicit treatment.
7. [Component](07-component.md) ‚Äî the `$()` callable and the Component getter. Class form returns the Component for the class. Instance form lifts a held instance. String form looks up an HTML catalogue entry. The lift-path on `$Particle` vs the template-path override on `$Chemical`.
8. [Perspectives](08-perspectives.md) ‚Äî the two orthogonal axes of perspective on `$Particle`. Horizontal: sibling subclasses `reveal` their `view`s onto a base, read back as instance-bound `Perspective` lenses for a menu. Vertical: `look(up/down)` walks one live instance up and down its own prototype chain to render through an ancestor view ‚Äî revert to base ‚Äî via the scope-tracked cursor and internal `$view`.
9. [The Composition of Perspectives](09-the-composition-of-perspectives.md) ‚Äî the design philosophy under the mechanics: a perspective is function composition with the view and its `instance` reified on one object (Doug's design), and inheritance is the valence ‚Äî is-a decides which (view, instance) pairings are legal. Covers the third axis (fix the view, swap the instance), the grid of legal pairings, perspectives as chemical reactions, why `this` fuses standpoint and state, and how to design features around all of it.
10. [Glossary](10-glossary.md) ‚Äî alphabetical reference of particle-layer terms, both perspective axes included: `$Particle`, `view()`, `$view`, `look()`, `viewLevel`, `perspectives`, `reveal()`, `Perspective`, `$apply$`, `isParticle`, particular, prototypal view.
