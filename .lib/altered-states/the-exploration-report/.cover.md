# The Exploration Report

- **subject:** [Altered States](../..altered-states/.cover.md)

How we turn a dataset into something a collaborator can actually understand — the **method** of a descriptive data-exploration report, not any one report's findings. [The Altered Cortex](../the-altered-cortex/.cover.md) holds the science and [Datasets](../datasets/.cover.md) the data; this book holds the craft of *looking and showing*: a set of figures that tell one honest story across both conditions, and a woven narrative — title, intro, motivation, each analysis with its math and its reason for mattering, and a conclusion — that a reader who never ran the analysis can follow. It exists because the first pass taught us the difference between decorating figures and *meaning* them: colour is a grammar and not a mood, every quantity earns a name, the honesty lives in the prose and never as alarms painted on the pixels, and a descriptive set is built to land on the confound it must survive. The worked example is the [Initial Data Exploration](../../reports/2026-06-23-exploration/initial-data-exploration.md); this book is the recipe behind it, edited as we learn to do it better.

**Synopsis.** The worked example reached its honest form only after the analysis settled: per-scan normalization makes the two scans comparable, the large behavioural change decodes at ~97% without leaving a gross artifact in the normalized code, and the gain change (the published DOI effect that normalization removes) is deferred to a within-scan scale-invariant analysis. Its final draft was written with a Desktop `/think` — Desktop supplying the prose, the team holding the judgment that it stays true to the figures — the cleanest instance of the recipe's own factorization: borrow the writing, keep the checking.
