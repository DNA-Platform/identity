The altered-states data as it actually sits on disk — the reference for what is loaded, in what shape, and with which caveats, kept apart from what the experiment *means* ([The Altered Cortex](../the-altered-cortex/.cover.md)) and how it is *run* ([The Build](../the-build/.cover.md)). The data is two two-photon calcium-imaging scans of a single mouse, **animal 33328**, in the Sensorium / neuralpredictors *static-scan* format: **session 6 / scan 2** is the **pre-DOI baseline** (1654 neurons, 5936 trials) and **session 7 / scan 1** is the **post-DOI** recording (1449 neurons, 5996 trials), the same GrayImageNet natural images shown in both. Because the two populations are independently segmented and differ in size, the conditions are bridged by a matched-cell index — about 749 cells located in both scans by reciprocal-nearest-neighbour registration within 10 µm — and the repeated test images give the like-for-like stimulus set. This book documents the two scans, the file format, the counts and tiers, the cell-matching step (now a verified core module), and the data-level caveats that bound what the analysis may claim. Every count here is read straight from the files; where a statement is interpretive it is marked, and the falsifiable hypotheses these numbers feed live in The Altered Cortex.

## Chapters

1. [The two scans](01-the-two-scans.md) — animal 33328, the pre-DOI (session 6 / scan 2) and post-DOI (session 7 / scan 1) recordings as **one continuous ~90-minute session** of **volumetric** two-photon imaging, the GrayImageNet stimulus, and why `scan_idx` is not the chronological order
2. [The static-scan format](02-the-static-scan-format.md) — the neuralpredictors FileTreeDataset layout: per-trial `.npy` files, the `meta/` trees for neurons and trials, the normalization statistics, and the relative-path gotcha
3. [Trials, tiers, and counts](03-trials-tiers-and-counts.md) — the per-scan numbers, the train / validation / test split, the 100 repeated test images and the noise ceiling they support, and the behavioral covariates
4. [Matching cells across the two scans](04-matching-cells.md) — `unit_stack_coords.csv`, the reciprocal-nearest-neighbour matcher, and the verified 749 matched pairs (median 2.68 µm) guarded by a regression test
5. [Caveats that bound the science](05-caveats.md) — no labelled stimulus-free epochs, the size mismatch between conditions, the arousal/locomotion confound, the volumetric **z-drift** that mimics a global dimming (and explains the ~50% re-match rate), and the fact that the shipped per-neuron normalization **removes exactly** the published DOI gain-reduction effect — so it needs scale-invariant within-session detection
