The paper's own book is [Functional Connectomics](../../papers/functional-connectomics-tolias-2024/.cover.md). What *we* took from it:

## What we learned

This paper shows **no MEIs and no reconstructed images**, so it does **not** inform our image-display recipe. Its figures are quantitative — Fig 6, for instance, is RNN correlation / lesion plots. Crucially, it uses the digital twin's **feature-weights as scalar similarities** — numbers fed into connectivity analyses — **never as pictures**. Here the twin is an instrument for measuring representational similarity, not a generator of images.

## What it informs

- **Methodology, not display.** Reading the twin's internal feature-weights as a *similarity quantity* is a precedent for interrogating the model's representation numerically; it is relevant wherever we compare population structure, not to how figures are rendered.
- It is deliberately **out of scope** for [the figure-display print](../projection/print--faithful-figure-display.md) — recorded here so the print does not spend a search looking for a display recipe this paper does not contain.
