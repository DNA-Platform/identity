---

This is the cataloguing book for the altered-states branch library. The project is a psychedelic exploration into altered states of consciousness, using mouse visual cortex as its lens. This branch records the team's applied knowledge of that exploration — the questions taken in, the decisions made, and what the work taught the team — not what any code does or how an API behaves. Altered States catalogues that knowledge as it accumulates.

This branch is the [identity layer](../../../.claude/library/bookkeeping/13-on-authorship.md#names-live-only-in-the-identity-layer) — `library/.lib`, where the team is named — and it catalogues the de-named project content beside it (the papers, Erin's overview deck) by pointing outward at it. The team is named here and never inside the project content; the links run one way, from `.lib` into the project.

## Books

### [Altered States](.cover.md) — Altered States

Self-cataloguing. The branch library knowing what it knows about the project — the team's understanding of exploring altered states of consciousness through the visual cortex, organized as the work produces it.

### [Projection](../projection/.cover.md)

The sprint-by-sprint record of the team's effort on altered-states — what each sprint aimed to do, what was built, and what was learned. The branch's autobiography, populated as the work proceeds.

### [The Altered Cortex](../the-altered-cortex/.cover.md)

The experiment made analyzable: the DOI dataset characterized from the files, the word "hallucination" turned into numbered falsifiable hypotheses each with its disproof and control, and the analysis plan grounded in the digital-twin encoding model and gradient inversion. The setup the analysis resumes from — same cells, same images, before and after the psychedelic.

### [Datasets](../datasets/.cover.md)

The data as it actually sits on disk: two scans of mouse 33328 (session 6/scan 2 pre-DOI, 1654 cells; session 7/scan 1 post-DOI, 1449 cells), the Sensorium static-scan format, the trials and tiers, the verified ~749 matched cells (median 2.68 µm), and the caveats that bound the science — the volumetric z-drift and the normalization/gain-change trap among them. The on-disk counterpart to The Altered Cortex's conceptual dataset — what we are analyzing. Authored by me from the data; Nancy is the domain source.

### [The Build](../the-build/.cover.md)

How the analysis is built and run: a side-by-side Python 3.11 via uv, the pip-tools-locked venv, the torch (CPU) + Sensorium-triple stack and the six failures it fought, the src/notebooks/results split anchored on the frozen digital-twin checkpoint, the disk-bound FileTreeDataset loading, and reproducibility. Authored by me from Adam's build; Adam is the domain source.

### [The Exploration Report](../the-exploration-report/.cover.md)

How we turn a dataset into something a collaborator can understand — the method of a descriptive data-exploration report, not any one report's findings: the sections (title, intro, motivation, each analysis with its math and its reason for mattering, conclusion), the figure discipline (one story across both conditions, colour-as-grammar, name everything, honesty in the prose not on the pixels), and the docstring → per-figure md → woven report pipeline. The living recipe behind the [Initial Data Exploration](../../reports/2026-06-23-exploration/initial-data-exploration.md); authored by Nancy from the craft of writing it.

### [The Literature](../the-literature/.cover.md)

What we learned from each paper we hold and what it informs — a research-book catalogue whose chapters point to the paper books in `library/papers/` rather than duplicating them: the [digital-twins twin + display recipe](../the-literature/01-digital-twins-tolias-2022.md), [functional-connectomics methodology](../the-literature/02-functional-connectomics-tolias-2024.md), and [sequence-filtering](../the-literature/03-sequence-filtering-histed-2025.md). It records our reading and the code or report each paper touched, so opening a paper again starts from what we already know. Authored by me; Nancy is the domain source.
