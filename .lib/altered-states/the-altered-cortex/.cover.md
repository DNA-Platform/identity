# The Altered Cortex

- **author:** [Nancy](../../../.claude/library/..teamsmanship/..team/nancy/nancy-or-the-weight-of-evidence/.cover.md)
- **subject:** [Altered States](../..altered-states/.cover.md)

---

The experiment, made analyzable. This book is the working record of the altered-states dataset and the plan to interrogate it: two-photon calcium imaging of the same mouse V1 population recorded twice — once before and once after the animal received DOI, a 5-HT2A agonist psychedelic — while the same natural images were shown both times. It documents what was actually recorded (verified against the files, not the walkthrough's prose), turns the project's animating word — *hallucination* — into a numbered set of falsifiable hypotheses each paired with the observation that would disprove it and the control it is measured against, and lays out an analysis plan grounded in the digital-twin encoding model and gradient inversion. Its discipline is the branch's discipline: a model that fits is not a mechanism, and what can be decoded is not what the animal sees. Read it before running anything; it is the context the analysis resumes from. It complements [Sprint 1](../projection/01-sprint-1--does-the-mouse-hallucinate.md), which states the goal and the tasks; this book holds the dataset, the hypotheses, and the method.

## Chapters

1. [The dataset and the design](01-the-dataset-and-the-design.md) — what was recorded and what the files actually contain: two matched-cell scans of animal 33328 (session 6 pre-DOI, session 7 post-DOI — the assignment confirmed from the protocol slides), the GrayImageNet stimulus, trials and tiers, behavior and pupil, the 749 cells tracked across both scans, the 100 repeated test images shared by both conditions, and what is and is not analyzable.
2. [The question, made falsifiable](02-the-question-made-falsifiable.md) — operational definitions of "hallucination" in V1 population data and seven numbered hypotheses, each with its disproof and the control (pre-DOI baseline, time-matched epoch, trial and identity shuffles) it is weighed against. The chapter where the exciting word is forced to make predictions it can fail.
3. [The analysis plan](03-the-analysis-plan.md) — the method, in order: build and validate the digital-twin encoding model on pre-DOI data, characterize tuning, reliability, and population geometry, re-run everything on the matched cells under DOI with the same images, test for structured stimulus-like spontaneous activity, and — only if it is found — invert the twin to reconstruct candidate hallucinated content, with the standing caveat that decodable is not experienced.
4. [Cataloguing the deck](04-cataloguing-the-deck.md) — Erin Neyhart's project-overview presentation, kept de-named in the project layer, read from inside the analysis: each slide-claim mapped to where it is made falsifiable, and the preliminary drug-state classifier read as the arousal/locomotion confound the whole analysis must survive. The rigorous counterpart to the orienting deck.
