# The question, made falsifiable

- **author:** [Nancy](../../../.claude/library/..teamsmanship/..team/nancy/nancy-or-the-weight-of-evidence/.cover.md)

---

"Does the mouse hallucinate?" is not yet a scientific question. It becomes one only when *hallucination* names something a recording can show and, just as importantly, something the recording could fail to show. A hypothesis that no observation could disprove is not a hypothesis. So every claim below is stated with the observation that would kill it and the control it is measured against. The default is the null: DOI changes nothing about the population code beyond what arousal, motion, and noise already change. The burden is on the alternative.

## Operationalizing "hallucination"

A hallucination, in the subjective sense, is a percept without a corresponding stimulus. We cannot observe the mouse's percept. What a V1 population recording can observe is *structured neural activity that resembles stimulus-evoked activity but is not driven by the current stimulus*. That is the operational stand-in, and it is deliberately weaker than the subjective claim — decodable structure is not experienced content, and this gap is never closed by this data. Three measurable construals follow, in increasing strength:

- **Weak — altered representation.** Under DOI the same images drive a measurably different population response than at baseline: tuning shifts, reliability drops, the geometry of the code changes. This is "perception is altered," not yet "perception is hallucinated."
- **Medium — internally structured activity.** Under DOI the population carries more low-dimensional, stimulus-like structure that is *not* explained by the shown image — the response contains organized content the stimulus did not put there.
- **Strong — decodable phantom content.** That extra structure, run through a baseline-trained decoder, reads out as a coherent image-like pattern rather than noise — the population "represents" something the eyes were not shown.

The project's claim is interesting at the medium and strong levels. The weak level is the necessary precondition and the easiest to establish; the strong level is the one to distrust most, because a decoder will always output *something*, and an expressive model can manufacture structure from noise. The controls exist to separate real signal from that manufacture.

## The controls, named once

Every hypothesis is weighed against one or more of these, so they are defined here rather than repeated:

- **Pre-DOI baseline.** The session-6 scan defines "normal" for this animal. Every post-DOI quantity is a *change* from its own baseline, not an absolute.
- **Matched cells.** Comparisons run on the 749 reciprocally matched neurons, so a change is in the same physical cells, not a different sample.
- **Matched stimulus.** The 100 shared, repeated test images let a fixed image's response be compared directly across conditions.
- **Trial shuffle.** Shuffling responses across trials within a condition destroys stimulus-locking while preserving marginal statistics — the floor for "this structure is stimulus-related."
- **Identity / label shuffle.** Permuting the pre/post label or the cell-matching destroys the condition effect while preserving everything else — the floor for "this is a DOI effect and not a registration or ordering artifact."
- **Behavioral nuisance regression.** Running speed and pupil are regressed out (or matched across conditions) so that arousal and movement, which DOI changes, are not mistaken for representational change. DOI's effect on the code must survive removal of its effect on state.

## The hypotheses

**H1 — Tuning is altered under DOI (weak construal).** For matched cells and matched images, per-neuron tuning curves and the population's stimulus encoding differ post-DOI from baseline beyond trial-to-trial variability. *Disproof:* the cross-condition response difference for matched images is within the within-condition noise (the difference between two halves of the baseline repeats), i.e. DOI is statistically indistinguishable from re-recording the baseline. *Controls:* matched cells, matched stimulus, pre-DOI baseline split-half as the noise reference, behavioral regression.

**H2 — Stimulus reliability decreases under DOI.** The trial-to-trial reliability of responses to repeated images (the noise ceiling / oracle) drops post-DOI — the code becomes noisier or less stimulus-locked, consistent with intrinsic activity competing with sensory drive. *Disproof:* per-neuron repeat reliability is equal or higher under DOI. *Controls:* matched cells, matched (repeated) images, identity shuffle to set the chance reliability floor. *Note:* this prediction follows from the Charles Bonnet "release/disinhibition" framing the protocol invokes — reduced sensory dominance — but lower reliability alone is also the boring signature of a sicker, drowsier, or more motion-contaminated animal; H2 is necessary, not sufficient, and is why behavioral regression is mandatory here.

**H3 — Population geometry changes under DOI.** The dimensionality and correlation structure of the population code shift post-DOI — e.g. higher shared variance, increased participation of a few dominant modes, or a rotation of the representational space relative to baseline. *Disproof:* dimensionality, the eigenspectrum of the noise and signal covariance, and representational similarity to baseline are unchanged within the shuffle-derived confidence band. *Controls:* pre-DOI baseline, matched cells, trial shuffle for the chance geometry, behavioral regression.

**H4 — The encoding model degrades under DOI (and degrades structurally, not uniformly).** A digital twin fit on pre-DOI data predicts post-DOI responses worse than it predicts held-out pre-DOI responses, and the residual (what the model cannot explain) is itself structured rather than white. *Disproof:* the model transfers to post-DOI with no loss in normalized correlation beyond the baseline test-set value, and its residuals are indistinguishable from noise. *Controls:* the model's own pre-DOI held-out test performance as the reference, matched cells, matched images, residual whiteness tested against a trial shuffle. *Why it matters:* H4 is the bridge from "the response changed" (H1) to "the response contains content the stimulus did not provide" (H5). Structured residuals are the first quantitative foothold for the hallucination claim.

**H5 — Activity under DOI carries stimulus-like structure not driven by the shown image (medium construal).** The component of the post-DOI population response unexplained by the current image is low-dimensional and image-like — it resembles evoked responses to *other* images more than it resembles noise. *Disproof:* the unexplained component is high-dimensional and statistically white — its similarity to the bank of real evoked responses is no greater than a trial-shuffled surrogate's. *Controls:* trial shuffle and identity shuffle as the null for "stimulus-like," the baseline residual structure as the comparison (baseline residuals should be closer to white), matched cells, behavioral regression. *Data caveat:* this is the hypothesis most constrained by [the dataset](01-the-dataset-and-the-design.md#what-is-and-is-not-analyzable) — there are no labelled stimulus-free epochs, so "not driven by the shown image" must be operationalized as the *residual* after the encoding model accounts for the image. The test is only as clean as that residual.

**H6 — Decoded phantom content is coherent, not arbitrary (strong construal).** A decoder (or the inverted twin) trained only on pre-DOI evoked activity, applied to the H5 residual structure under DOI, produces image-like reconstructions that are more coherent and more mutually consistent than what the same decoder produces from baseline residuals or from shuffled activity. *Disproof:* reconstructions from DOI residuals are no more coherent — by any blind, pre-registered measure — than reconstructions from baseline residuals or from trial-shuffled activity, i.e. the decoder hallucinates equally from nothing. *Controls:* baseline-residual reconstructions, trial-shuffle reconstructions, and identity-shuffle reconstructions as three nulls; the reconstruction quality on real held-out images as the ceiling. *Standing caveat, in bold because it is the whole point:* a reconstruction is what the *model* would need to see to reproduce the activity; it is not what the *animal* sees. Decodable is not experienced. H6 can at most license the sentence "the altered population code is organized like a representation of structured visual content," never "the mouse saw X."

**H7 — The effect tracks the drug, not the passage of time or the state.** The H1–H6 signatures are specifically attributable to DOI: they exceed what time-matched re-recording, arousal change, and motion would produce on their own. *Disproof:* the signatures are reproduced by within-baseline contrasts (early vs late baseline, high- vs low-arousal baseline epochs) at the same magnitude — i.e. anything that differs between two blocks would have produced them. *Controls:* within-baseline split contrasts as a time/state placebo, behavioral matching across conditions, identity (label) shuffle. This is the hypothesis that protects every other one from the most likely confound, and the one whose failure would most cleanly retire the whole claim.

## How the answer is reported

The honest outcomes are three: *for* (H1 and H2/H3 hold, H4/H5 show structured residuals, H6 survives its nulls, H7 attributes it to DOI), *against* (the chain breaks at H4/H5 — changes are real but not stimulus-like, or break at H7 — they are time/state effects), or *inconclusive* (the weak construal holds but the data, especially the absence of labelled spontaneous epochs, cannot reach the medium construal). Each is a defensible result. The discipline is to report the level the evidence actually reaches and no higher — and to state, every time the strong construal is in play, that decodable is not experienced. [The analysis plan](03-the-analysis-plan.md) is the order of operations that produces these tests.
